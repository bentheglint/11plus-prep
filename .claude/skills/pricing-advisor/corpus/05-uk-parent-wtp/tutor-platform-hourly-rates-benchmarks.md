## Source

Four major UK tutoring platforms and one premium agency publish regular data on prevailing tutor hourly rates. This data represents the actual market prices parents encounter when booking tutors, making it the most direct benchmark for what a digital 11+ prep app is competing against.

Sources:
- **Tutorful** — UK's largest marketplace platform, with 90% grade-improvement claim. Blog posts on maths, English, science tutor costs (updated 2024/2025).
- **TutorCruncher** — tutor management software; publishes aggregated rate data from its platform users. Updated guide on average tutoring rates UK (2026 edition).
- **Bark** — lead-generation marketplace for tutors. Pricing guide updated 2025.
- **The Profs** — premium agency. Their data on 11-plus rates was cited in Atom Learning and Telegraph coverage (Oct 2023–Jan 2024 figures).
- **SaveMyExams** — secondary source compiling 2025 tutor cost data.

## Key findings (the headline numbers)

**Overall UK average (all subjects, all ages):**
- Tutorful: **£37.45/hour** average across all tutors on platform (2024/25)
- TutorCruncher aggregated data: approximately **£40/hour** average across UK

**By education level (Tutorful, 2024/25):**
| Level | Hourly rate |
|---|---|
| Primary school | £27.87 |
| KS3 | £32.23 |
| GCSE | £34.23 |
| A Level | £41.50 |
| IB | £48.38 |
| Degree | £52.74 |

**11+ specific (Tutorful, 2024/25):**
- Children nearing end of primary preparing for 11+: **£41.12/hour** average
- English for 11+ prep: **~£37/hour**
- Online maths tuition (all primary/KS2): **£35.29/hour**; in-person: **£41.75/hour**

**Regional variation (TutorCruncher 2026 data — all subjects):**
| Region | Rate |
|---|---|
| Hampshire | £77.68 |
| London | £75.55 |
| Surrey | £74.89 |

| Warwickshire | £70.10 |

Note: TutorCruncher figures appear to cover all tutor experience levels and subjects including specialist/senior; the Tutorful figures (which segment by level) appear more grounded for the 11+ primary segment. The Mumsnet and Atom data (£40–£90/hour range for 11+ specifically) aligns with Tutorful's level-specific figures rather than TutorCruncher's aggregate.

**The Profs (premium agency, 11+ specific):**
- Oct 2023–Jan 2024: **£91.25/hour average** for 11-plus tutoring
- Previous year (Oct 2022–Jan 2023): **£80/hour**
- Year-on-year increase: **~14%**

**Bark (marketplace, 2025 pricing guide):**
- Group tutoring: £10–£15/session
- Individual: £25–£40/session
- Premium/expert: £60–£100/session
- Key Stage 2 (ages 7–11): **£18–£20** average listed; note this appears to be the entry-level / student-tutor end of their marketplace

**Frequency and total cost:**
- Typical preparation programme: 1 hour/week, 50 sessions over 12 months at UK average (~£55/hour for specialist 11+ tutor) = **£2,750**
- Realistic all-in (including materials, mock exams): **£3,000–5,000**
- Extended (2 years, multiple subjects): **£5,000–8,000**
- Home-based self-study (no tutor, structured materials + mock exams only): **£350–600 total**

## Sample / methodology (if known)

- **Tutorful**: Data drawn from actual transactions on their marketplace platform. This is real transaction data, not survey estimates. Updated quarterly.
- **TutorCruncher**: Aggregated from their tutor management software clients — actual invoiced rates. 2026 edition.
- **Bark**: Self-reported pricing guidance for their lead-generation marketplace. Less rigorous than transaction data.
- **The Profs**: Premium agency with higher-end client base; their 11+ figures represent the upper end of the market.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

The platform rate data establishes a clear competitive frame. The prevailing market rate for a specialist 11+ tutor is £40–£90/hour depending on region and format. At even one session per week, this costs parents £160–£360/month. An app at £40–£70/month is therefore 10–50% of one month's tutoring spend — positioning it as a supplement (not replacement) rather than a competitive threat, which is a more comfortable purchase decision. For price-sensitive parents (Bark's marketplace entry-level, Mumsnet's North West reports), even £15–£25/month is still framed against a £25–£35/hour tutor market. The absence of regulation and wide rate variance means parents have no reliable mental anchor for "fair" tutoring costs — creating an opportunity for transparent, fixed-price app subscriptions to signal fairness and predictability.
