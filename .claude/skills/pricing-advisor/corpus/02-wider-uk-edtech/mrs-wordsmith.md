## Overview

Mrs Wordsmith is a vocabulary and literacy app aimed at children aged 6–11 (Key Stage 1–2 in the UK). It uses cinematic, character-driven storytelling and original artwork to teach word roots, definitions, and usage through games, illustrated books, and short films. The product spans a companion app (iOS/Android) and physical book ranges. Its primary differentiator is high-production-value creative content rather than curriculum drill. It targets parents who want enrichment alongside school, and has been adopted in some US and UK schools. Subject focus is strictly English vocabulary and reading comprehension.

## Family / sibling pricing

Up to 4 child accounts are bundled into a single subscription at no additional cost. There is no published sibling or multi-child add-on beyond this bundled allowance. This is competitive value for families with 2–4 children.

## Schools licensing (if applicable)

Mrs Wordsmith does sell to schools — physical book sets and classroom licences have been offered through partnership arrangements — but no public per-pupil school pricing page exists. School enquiries are handled directly. This limits scalability as a self-serve school channel.

## Unusual mechanics

- **30% vocabulary improvement guarantee:** Secondary sources cite a claim that children who use the app as directed will achieve measurable vocabulary improvement; exact terms and conditions were not retrievable at time of writing.
- **4-accounts-per-subscription:** Unusually generous household bundling for the price point.
- **USD-only pricing:** The absence of a GBP storefront is a friction point for UK parents who encounter FX uncertainty at checkout.
- **Physical / digital hybrid:** The brand sells illustrated books and workbooks alongside the digital subscription, which creates an additional revenue layer and higher brand presence.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

The 4-accounts-per-subscription model at ~£7.90/month is a benchmark for family-bundle generosity. For a UK 11+ app, offering a 2–3 child household plan at a modest uplift (rather than per-child add-ons) could be similarly compelling to the target parent demographic.
