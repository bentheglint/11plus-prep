## Source

The Department for Education (DfE) published annual statistics on the National Tutoring Programme (NTP) through the Explore Education Statistics portal. The NTP ran from November 2020 to August 2024, when it was defunded. Ofsted was commissioned to carry out an independent review of the NTP, with Phase 1 findings published in 2023.

The NTP is directly relevant to UK parent willingness-to-pay because:
1. It established state-subsidised tutoring cost benchmarks.
2. Its closure in August 2024 removed school-based tutoring from 1+ million pupils per year, increasing the market gap that private providers and apps now fill.
3. Its data on which pupils received tutoring illuminates unmet demand.

## Key findings (the headline numbers)

**Programme scale (2023–24 final year):**
- **1.07 million pupils** received tutoring through the NTP in 2023–24 (down from 1.47 million in 2022–23). (DfE, NTP 2023-24 statistics)
- **16.4 million hours** of tutoring delivered in 2023–24. (DfE)
- Average course duration: **10.9 hours** per pupil. (DfE)
- **6.17 million total course starts** delivered across all four programme years (2020–2024). (DfE)

**School participation:**
- **59.4%** of schools participated in 2023–24, down from **73.8%** in 2022–23. (DfE)
- Secondary schools had higher participation (**78.5%**) than primary schools (**56.3%**) and other schools (**50.6%**). (DfE)

**Pupil characteristics (May 2024):**
- **45.2%** of NTP pupils were eligible for free school meals in the previous 6 years.
- **28.1%** had special educational needs.

**Cost/subsidy structure (2023–24):**
- Subsidy covered **up to 50%** of tuition costs for pupil-premium-eligible students.
- Minimum per-pupil subsidy in mainstream schools: **£67.50 per eligible pupil**.
- Non-mainstream schools: minimum **£176.25 per eligible pupil**.

**What the NTP closure means for demand:**
- The Sutton Trust's 2026 report found that **58% of schools** reduced their tutoring offer in 2025 (the first full year post-NTP). In-school tutoring fell from 22% of pupils in 2023 to **20%** in 2026.
- Pre-NTP (early 2020): only **10%** of secondary school leaders prioritised one-to-one or small group tuition for Pupil Premium spending. By 2022 this had risen to **34%**. Following the programme's end, this proportion is declining.

## Sample / methodology (if known)

DfE statistics are administrative data collected from schools participating in the NTP. Published via the Explore Education Statistics portal. Considered authoritative official government data. The Ofsted review was based on inspections of NTP provision and interviews with tutors, teachers, and pupils over a two-year period from September 2021.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

The NTP created a temporary baseline expectation: school-based tutoring was available to disadvantaged pupils at subsidised rates of roughly £5–£7/hour effective cost. The programme's closure in August 2024 removed this for over a million pupils per year. This creates a structural market gap — particularly for primary-age pupils (the 11+ target demographic) where school participation was lower (56.3%). An app at £40–£70/month represents roughly 2–4 weeks of NTP-equivalent tutoring hours at market rates, but as an always-on resource covering the full year. The closure of the NTP also means there is no longer a competing "free" state alternative for most pupils, strengthening the value proposition.
