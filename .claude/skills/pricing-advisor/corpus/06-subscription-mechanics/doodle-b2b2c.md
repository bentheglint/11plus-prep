## What this covers

Doodle Learning (formerly Doodle Maths) is a UK-based adaptive learning platform covering maths, English, spelling, and times tables for primary-age children. It operates a dual-channel B2B2C model — selling per-pupil licences to schools while running a parallel family subscription business — and publishes some of the clearest documented pricing in UK edtech. The contrast between its school per-pupil rate and its family per-child rate illustrates the structural mechanics of B2B2C pricing asymmetry with unusual transparency.

The previous research brief cited £159.99/year for the bundle family plan; this could not be confirmed from the current help article. The £119.99 figure is used where confirmed.

**School pricing:**

| Plan | Per pupil / year |
|---|---|
| DoodleMaths + DoodleTables | £6/pupil |
| DoodleEnglish + DoodleSpell | £6/pupil |
| Full bundle (all four apps) | £9/pupil |

Minimum subscription applies; schools in multi-academy trusts receive further discounts.

**The per-pupil price asymmetry:**

This is the defining feature of the Doodle model:

- School bundle: **£9/pupil/year**
- Family single child, annual: **£69.99/year** (= £69.99/pupil)
- Family bundle, up to 6 children, annual: **£119.99/year** (= £20.00/child if fully utilised)

The school price (£9/pupil) is approximately 7.8x cheaper than the family single-child annual (£69.99). Even the per-child family rate at maximum utilisation (£20.00) is 2.2x the school rate.

This asymmetry is standard B2B2C economics — schools receive bulk pricing in exchange for access to their pupil population, which becomes the lead generation funnel for family subscriptions. The school price does not need to cover costs independently; it needs only to be below the marginal cost of acquiring that parent through any other channel.

**Annual discount on family plans:**

Single child monthly vs annual:
- Monthly: £7.99 × 12 = £95.88
- Annual: £69.99
- Discount: **27%** — higher than the Sacks-recommended 15–20% band

Family monthly vs annual:
- Monthly: £12.99 × 12 = £155.88
- Annual: £119.99
- Discount: **23%**

Doodle runs annual discounts above the canonical SaaS band, prioritising cash collection and annual commitment over margin.

**Lifetime plans:**

Doodle offers lifetime plans (£179 single child, £299 family), which are unusual in subscription edtech. These serve a specific customer segment: parents who have calculated that their child will use the platform for 3+ years and want to pay once. At £179/lifetime vs £69.99/year, the lifetime plan breaks even at 2.6 years — a reasonable bet for a family with a child in Year 2 who will be in Year 5 at the end of the window. Lifetime plans also eliminate the renewal friction that causes churn.

**The B2B funnel in practice:**

Doodle's school penetration is substantial — it is used in thousands of UK primary schools. The funnel operates:

1. School purchases per-pupil licences → children use Doodle in class and as homework
2. Parents see child using it → prompted to explore home access
3. Doodle markets home subscriptions to parents via school communication channels (letters home, teacher recommendations)
4. Parent converts to family subscription at £69.99–£119.99/year

The school licence effectively pre-qualifies the parent: by the time they reach the family pricing page, they have seen the product in action through their child's use and have already formed a view of its quality.

**Discovery Education connection:**

Doodle Learning was acquired by Discovery Education in 2021, the same group that subsequently acquired TTRS. Discovery Education's B2B sales infrastructure has accelerated Doodle's school penetration significantly since the acquisition.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app

Doodle's model provides the clearest pricing architecture template for a UK 11+ app: a £6–£9/pupil/year school tier, a £69.99–£79.99/year single-child home tier, and a £119.99–£129.99/year family tier (up to 3 children). These price points are already validated in the market and sit at a meaningful discount to Atom Learning (which charges £383–£671/year per child). A challenger that matches Doodle's per-child pricing on a single-subject basis but bundles verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, English, and maths into a single 11+ platform can compete with Atom on value at half the price. The lifetime plan mechanic is worth considering for a launch cohort — it converts price-sensitive early adopters into committed users whose engagement strengthens the product over time.
