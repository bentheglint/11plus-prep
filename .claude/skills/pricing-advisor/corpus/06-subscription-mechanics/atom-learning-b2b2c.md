## What this covers

Atom Learning is the UK's leading 11+ preparation platform and the closest direct comparable to an independent 11+ app. Its B2B2C model — free school access funded by parent home subscriptions — is the most studied implementation of this mechanic in the UK edtech market. This file documents Atom's verified 2026 pricing, the virtuous cycle that powers the model, and what a challenger app can learn from it.

## The pattern / mechanic

**Verified Atom Learning pricing (May 2026):**

Home (parent-facing) subscription tiers for ages 7–11+:

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (billed annually) | Annual saving |
|---|---|---|---|
| EMS (English, Maths, Science) | £39.99/mo | £31.99/mo (£383.90/yr) | £95.98 (~20%) |
| Exam Prep | £59.99/mo | £47.99/mo (£575.90/yr) | £143.98 (~20%) |
| Exam Prep Plus (+ unlimited mocks) | £69.99/mo | £55.99/mo (£671.90/yr) | £167.98 (~20%) |

GCSE plans (student-facing):

| Plan | Monthly | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Free | £0 | Limited practice, instant marking |
| Pro | £49.99/mo | Unlimited questions, past papers, predicted grades |

School access (Atom School): free to schools. Schools get full platform access at no cost.

**The virtuous cycle:**

Atom's B2B2C flywheel operates as follows:

1. Schools are onboarded at no cost → teachers recommend Atom as part of their 11+ preparation guidance
2. Parents of those children discover Atom through the school → high-trust acquisition (teacher recommendation, not advertising)
3. Parents subscribe to Atom Home → revenue is generated
4. Atom Home revenue funds the free school tier → flywheel continues

This is structurally similar to Slack's free team tier (which converts organisations to paid) but inverted: instead of converting the institution, Atom converts the households the institution introduces to the product.

**The per-child economics:**

Atom prices per child — each child requires a separate subscription. There is no family plan in Atom's current offering (as of May 2026). This creates a price cliff for families with multiple children in the 11+ window simultaneously: two children on Exam Prep = £119.98/month (monthly) or £1,151.80/year (annual). This is a structural gap a competitor could exploit with a family plan.

**Annual discount consistency:**

Atom applies a consistent 20% annual discount across all tiers. This is at the top of the Sacks-recommended 15–20% band, suggesting Atom has found the 20% level to be the effective threshold for shifting parent behaviour from monthly to annual billing.

**The mock test positioning:**

The separation between "Exam Prep" (£47.99/month annual) and "Exam Prep Plus" (£55.99/month annual) — a £96/year premium — is built around unlimited mock tests with instant marking. This is a deliberate upsell architecture: mock tests are the highest-anxiety product in 11+ prep (parents want to know exactly how their child will perform on the day), and Atom has isolated that value into a premium tier. Competitors who include mocks in a base tier are likely leaving upsell revenue on the table.

**Atom Tuition:**

Atom also offers one-to-one tutoring at £54/lesson, positioning this as a premium add-on above the software subscription. This creates a three-tier value ladder: free (school), subscription (£31.99–£55.99/month annual), tutoring (£54/session).

**Geographic positioning:**

Atom is UK-native and explicitly designed for the 11+ examination system. This makes its pricing the most directly comparable reference point for any UK 11+ challenger. The £31.99–£55.99/month annual range defines the premium tier of the UK market.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app

A challenger app has two strategic options against Atom's pricing: (a) undercut on the family tier (Atom has no family plan, so a £29.99/month for up to three children plan is priced below Atom's per-child rate while generating higher per-family ARPU than a single-child subscriber), or (b) mirror the Atom structure and compete on product quality. The B2B2C flywheel is replicable but requires sales effort to place in schools — a high-value investment with a 6–12 month payoff horizon. The free school tier should be a genuine product, not a cut-down demo, or teachers will not recommend it.
