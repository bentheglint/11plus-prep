## 1. Executive summary

PrepStep is a genuinely good product with, right now, almost no one arriving to see it. The bottleneck is not the app, the pricing, or the positioning. It is the **top of the funnel**: ~540 site visits a month, of which real cold-stranger discovery is ~50. Zero of those have become paying customers. So the entire job for the next two quarters is **acquisition + first conversions**, not retention or referral loops (which multiply a paying base that does not yet exist).

**The three bets:**

1. **Tutors are the wedge to the first 100 paid.** The single biggest non-homepage page on the site last month was *one tutor's referral link* (150 views, 3√ó all search traffic combined). Six tutors have signed up; exactly one invite has been sent. Tutors are free to reach, aligned by a 25% recurring commission, each bring a *cohort* of pre-qualified families, and structurally route around the local-rivalry problem that has sunk our parent-to-parent loops. **This is the fastest, cheapest path to revenue.**

2. **VOC-driven organic content + SEO is the compounding engine for 100 ‚Üí 1,000.** The `/practice` programmatic pages are built but pull ~50 search visits/month, so they are effectively not ranking. Fix that (indexing/ranking audit), then publish content that answers the questions parents actually type ("where is my child losing marks", "is an 11+ tutor worth it", "have I left 11+ prep too late", "grammar school instead of private after VAT"). Slow to start, but the only free channel that scales.

3. **Honesty + diagnosis, timed to the VAT tailwind, is the message that converts both.** Parents shop for a *diagnosis* ("where exactly is she losing marks"), not more questions, and they actively distrust tools that flatter (Atom's named weakness: app scores higher than real mocks). PrepStep's honest, non-inflated progress and teach-first method is a real wedge. And a fresh, quantified acquisition trigger is live: parents are switching from private school to grammar because of the 20% VAT on fees ("we save ¬£30k a year").

**90-day priorities:** (1) activate the tutor channel with a proper outreach + onboarding asset; (2) diagnose and fix why SEO isn't pulling, then ship the first VOC-intent content; (3) plug the silent trial‚Üípaid leak with a lifecycle email sequence (Resend is already wired, this is free and probably absent today); (4) sharpen the landing page to lead with diagnosis.

**12-month outcome (realistic, bootstrapped):** a working, measurable acquisition loop through tutors delivering the first cohort of paying families (path to 100), with a compounding content/SEO base beginning to contribute, and the first real testimonials in hand to unlock credible CRO and PR. Paid acquisition stays off the table until revenue funds it.

## 2. Strategic frame

**Category claim.** "11+ prep that teaches before it tests." The one tool that does the three things a good tutor does ‚Äî teaches the method, finds the weak spots, explains every wrong answer ‚Äî for less than one hour of tutoring a year.

**ICP (distilled).** UK parents (mums *and* dads; dads increasingly motivated post-VAT) of a child in **Year 4 or 5** preparing for the **GL Assessment** 11+ nationally. Anxious about a high-stakes one-shot exam, cost-conscious versus ¬£50‚Äì75/hr tutoring, and ‚Äî critically ‚Äî they want a *diagnosis*, not more drilling. Secondary audience: **private 11+ tutors** as a distribution channel (B2B2C).

**Business-model logic.** Freemium subscription. Free Forever (10 q/day) as the always-open door; ¬£24.99/mo or ¬£199/yr full access; 30-day full trial with no card. ARPC ‚âà ¬£199‚Äì300/yr. At ¬£0 paid budget, the model must grow on owned + earned + channel (tutor) motion, reinvesting revenue into paid only once CAC is known.

**Brand voice non-negotiables (VOC-anchored).** Warm, calm, plain, parent-to-parent. The audience's register is **guilt and "the system is brutal/unfair", not excitement** ‚Äî lead with empathy and diagnosis, never gamified hype. Honest to a fault (no fake testimonials, no ads, no card tricks). British English. **No em dashes in consumer copy.** Words to use: *losing marks, exam technique, timing, confidence, worth it*. Words to avoid: *cram, drilling, guaranteed pass, gimmick, inflated readiness*.

**What makes this plan different from the last six weeks.** We had been designing referral and retention loops (cash bounty, progress card, Prep Together) on a base of zero paying customers. This plan reorders to the actual constraint: get the right eyeballs, prove the first conversions, *then* switch the loops back on.

## 4. Acquisition (strangers ‚Üí aware)

The constraint is here. On ¬£0 and ~3‚Äì6h/wk, we run **two non-competing fronts** plus a low-effort earned play.

### Channel A ‚Äî Tutors (B2B2C). PRIMARY. Serves: first 100 paid.
*Why:* proven (150 views from one link), free, aligned (25% recurring), each tutor brings a cohort, and it routes around parent rivalry. Low weekly hours, relationship-driven ‚Äî fits a solo founder.
**90-day moves:**
- Reactivate the 6 dormant tutors: personal message, a reason to share, a frictionless link. (Skill: `referrals`.)
- Build a **tutor outreach + onboarding asset**: a one-page "why offer PrepStep to your families" (free to you, earn 25%, pupils arrive diagnosed) + a 30-second setup. (Skills: `copywriting`, `cro`; Fable reviews voice.)
- Recruit new tutors where they gather: tutoring directories, Facebook tutor groups, local 11+ tutor networks, Tutorful/MyTutor-style communities. Target: outreach to 10‚Äì15 tutors/week in the founder's ~2‚Äì3h.
- Instrument it: which tutor link drives signups ‚Üí paid (D1 + Stripe). Prove the loop.
**12-month:** a repeatable tutor-recruitment motion (a dozen active tutors, each with a handful of families) as the backbone of the first 100 paid.

### Channel B ‚Äî Organic content + SEO. SECONDARY. Serves: 100 ‚Üí 1,000 (compounding).
*Why:* the only free channel that scales; slow-burn; largely offloadable to Claude.
**90-day moves:**
- `seo-audit` on `/practice`: is it indexed, ranking, click-getting? Diagnose the ~50 visits/mo. (Skill: `seo-audit`; check Search Console.)
- Shift from thin templated pages to **VOC-intent content** answering real parent questions: "where is my child losing marks in 11+", "is an 11+ tutor worth it", "have I left 11+ prep too late (Year 5)", "grammar school vs private after VAT", "how the GL 11+ actually works". (Skills: `content-strategy`, `programmatic-seo`, `ai-seo`, `copywriting`; content by the 11+ Oracle where it's exam-substantive.)
- `ai-seo`: structure content to be cited by ChatGPT/Perplexity (parents increasingly ask AI). Low-cost edge.
**12-month:** 20‚Äì30 intent-matched pieces + fixed programmatic pages beginning to compound organic traffic.

### Channel C ‚Äî Earned / PR + Community. OPPORTUNISTIC. Serves: awareness spikes + trust.
- **PR angle (live, quotable):** the VAT-on-fees ‚Üí grammar surge. Pitch parenting press / education desks / newsletters with a founder-parent angle + the "¬£30k saved" story. (Skill: `public-relations`, HARO/Featured.)
- **Community (value, not spam):** be genuinely useful in Mumsnet / FB 11+ groups ‚Äî answer "where is she losing marks" questions with real help, brand by association not by pitching. (Skill: `community-marketing`.) This also feeds more VOC and future testimonials.

### Explicitly NOT doing now (and why)
- **Paid ads** ‚Äî no budget; and no known CAC to spend against. Revisit once revenue funds a test and one organic channel proves conversion.
- **App Store / ASO** ‚Äî PrepStep is a web app; not applicable.
- **Referral/viral loops as an acquisition channel** ‚Äî deferred to ¬ß7; they need a paying base.

## 10. 12-month outlook

Growth will be linear and lumpy, not a hockey stick. Sequenced S-curves:
- **Q1 (0‚Üífirst paid):** tutor channel + activation email carry it. Prove the loop. Get first testimonials.
- **Q2 (‚Üí toward 100 paid):** scale tutor recruitment; content begins to rank; first testimonials power landing CRO. Consider a *tiny* paid test **only if** revenue exists and one organic channel shows conversion.
- **Q3 (100 ‚Üí scaling):** content/SEO S-curve contributes real traffic; switch on retention basics (churn-prevention) and revisit parent referral (progress card / Prep Together) now that a base exists.
- **Q4 (toward 1,000):** compounding content + tutor network + referral loop layering; reinvest revenue into the first paid channel with a known CAC.

**Funding-stage note:** bootstrapped, no raise planned. So there is no "budget unlocks at round close" ‚Äî budget unlocks at *revenue*. The plan is deliberately organic-first for that reason. If a raise ever became an option, the first unlock would be a paid-acquisition test (once CAC is known) and the first contractor (content or outreach), not a hire.
