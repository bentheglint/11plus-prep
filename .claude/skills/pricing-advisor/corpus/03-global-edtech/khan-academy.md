## Overview

Khan Academy is a non-profit edtech organisation founded by Sal Khan in 2006, with a mission of "free, world-class education for anyone, anywhere." It serves over 150 million registered users globally and is used in tens of thousands of schools. The platform covers K–12 and beyond across maths, science, computing, humanities, and test prep (SAT, LSAT, MCAT). Khan Academy's pricing model is structurally different from commercial edtech: the core product is free by mission, not as a growth tactic, funded by donations (from Gates Foundation, Google, and individual donors) and, increasingly, by a paid AI tutor add-on called Khanmigo.

## Pricing tiers

**Khan Academy Core**
- Price: £0 / $0
- Billing: Free forever
- Included: Full curriculum content, exercises, videos, progress tracking, teacher and parent tools, course mastery system, SAT prep (in partnership with College Board).

**Khanmigo (AI Tutor)**
- Price: $4/month billed annually ($44/year, approx. £35/year); available for up to 10 child accounts under one parent account
- Billing: Annual only (as of early 2026)
- Included: Socratic AI tutoring (the AI asks questions rather than giving answers), writing coach, debate partner, AI-powered lesson planning tools for teachers, parent dashboard with child conversation summaries, content filters. Khanmigo uses GPT-4 and is designed to never simply give answers — it guides students to work through problems themselves.

**For Schools / Districts**
- Price: Custom / donation model for teachers; Khanmigo for teachers is currently offered free or at reduced cost to encourage adoption.
- Included: Teacher-facing AI tools: lesson planning, progress reports, differentiation suggestions.

## Free vs paid (the core question for global edtech)

Khan Academy's philosophy is the most extreme version of "free as mission": the entire curriculum is free regardless of ability to pay, forever, by the organisation's founding charter. There is no freemium conversion target. Khanmigo is the first paid product in Khan Academy's history, and it was introduced cautiously — Sal Khan has been publicly explicit that Khanmigo will never replace the free product and that the AI tutor is an enhancement, not a gate.

The Khanmigo model is notable because it flips the usual premium model: the paid layer adds a qualitatively different experience (an AI Socratic tutor) rather than removing friction or unlocking content. This is closer to a "premium service layer" than a typical SaaS upgrade.

## Family / multi-user pricing

The $44/year Khanmigo subscription covers one parent account and up to 10 child profiles. This is an exceptionally generous multi-child policy compared to any commercial competitor. It is likely subsidised by Khan Academy's non-profit structure and donor base rather than being economically self-sustaining at that price point. There is no separate "family plan" — the base Khanmigo subscription is inherently family-friendly.

## Unusual mechanics

- **Donation model for core product**: Khan Academy explicitly asks users who can afford to donate $5–10/month to do so, framing it as supporting access for students who cannot pay. This creates a social-good framing around payment that is unusually powerful for user trust.
- **Annual-only Khanmigo**: There is no monthly billing option for Khanmigo as of early 2026. This reduces churn and simplifies cash flow for a non-profit operating at low margins.
- **Socratic AI constraint**: Khanmigo is deliberately constrained to never give direct answers. This is both a pedagogical choice and a positioning differentiator — it is explicitly "not ChatGPT for homework."
- **US-market focus for paid features**: Khanmigo is currently US-only, with no announced UK launch date. This creates a gap in the global market for AI-tutored test prep — which a UK 11+ product could partially fill.
- **No ads, ever**: Khan Academy carries no advertising, which strengthens parent trust considerably.

## Positioning copy

Khan Academy's core positioning is mission-driven: "A free, world-class education for anyone, anywhere." Khanmigo is positioned as "your AI-powered tutor, guide, and learning companion." The emphasis is on the Socratic method: "Khanmigo won't just give you the answer — it'll help you figure it out yourself." Parent-facing copy emphasises safety and educational integrity: "Designed to help your child learn, not just get answers."

## Lessons for a UK 11+ app at smaller scale

Khan Academy demonstrates that a "free forever" core curriculum is achievable and trust-building even at enormous scale, though it requires donor funding that a small commercial product cannot replicate — so the lesson is about positioning rather than economics. The Khanmigo Socratic constraint (never give direct answers) is a design pattern directly relevant to 11+ prep, where the goal is exam readiness, not answer retrieval. The $44/year for 10 child accounts sets a reference anchor for family pricing that UK parents may use as a comparison point, meaning a £35–50/year single-child plan reads as competitive rather than cheap.
