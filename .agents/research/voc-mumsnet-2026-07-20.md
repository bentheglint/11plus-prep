# Voice-of-Customer Research — UK 11+ Parents (Mumsnet)

**Date:** 2026-07-20
**Source:** Mumsnet 11+ threads (2022–2026), fetched directly. Reddit had no indexed 11+ parent threads; elevenplusexams.co.uk forum returned 403 (recommend a manual visit — more prep-specific audience).
**Use:** feeds `.agents/product-marketing.md` (Customer Language, Objections, Brand Voice). NOT PrepStep-customer testimonials — this is the *market's* voice. Real customer quotes still to be collected by Ben.

## Strongest patterns (synthesis)
1. **Parents shop for a DIAGNOSIS, not more questions** — "where exactly is my child losing marks" is the single most repeated need. Stronger angle than "thousands of questions".
2. **Trust is fragile; parents distrust tools that flatter.** Atom's named failure: app scores higher than real mocks. Honest, conservative progress = a differentiator.
3. **Timing & technique ≠ knowledge.** "Ran out of time", "guessed the last five" recur as the real reason for underperformance.
4. **VAT-on-fees is a live, quantified acquisition trigger** — parents switching private→grammar, "save £30k a year". Fresh 2026 national hook.
5. **Emotional register = guilt & "brutal/unfair", not excitement.** Lead with empathy + calm honesty, not gamified hype.

## Verbatim quotes by theme

### Stakes / anxiety / guilt
- "One exam, on one day, to determine if you're 'grammar appropriate'. Bonkers." — 11 plus fears
- "I feel I have failed him." / "I would be playing some game of chance." — 11 plus shall we stop?
- "I honestly spent most of the time trying not to cry." / "I feel so guilty for choosing an area that has the Grammar system" — 11 plus support 2022
- "Seems so much rests on just one morning at 10 years old!" — support 2022
- "brutal... unfair system"; "I felt ashamed of myself for putting her through this" — 11 plus fears

### Tutors (cost / value)
- Rates reported: £75/hr (N London), £60/hr (SW London/Surrey), £50/hr group, £30/hr 1:1 (NW). — £45–60 per hour thread
- "an expensive babysitter and a fraud" (a bad tutor); "They need familiarisation, test technique and confidence." — 11+ tutor AMA
- "Practice materials on their own won't make him okay for the 11+ if he's not being taught the concepts alongside exam technique." — tutor AMA

### Apps (Atom / Bond / CGP / Century)
- Atom praise: "it gives the answers and WHY which is so much better than a lot of the books"; "see at a glance what your child has done that week."
- Atom distrust: "Atom was marking her prep work higher than she was achieving in her mocks"; "whether it is worth £600 and numerous hours".
- Cheaper alternatives parents volunteer: YoungLearning, ace11plus.org, "CGP and Bond books are honestly plenty for most kids".

### What good prep looks like
- "there's no point in being able to get the answers right if you can't get them down on paper in time."
- "It's really important that your DD learns to move on from a tricky question."
- "dc that were not tutored did not cover certain topics. This showed up in exams."

### Objections / scepticism
- "too much pressure on children and only benefit the affluent due to the emphasis on prep".
- "Both my children failed 11+. They are both in top sets at the state secondary."
- "not whether she's capable... but whether she would actually enjoy it" (fit/wellbeing, not just capability).

### VAT / private-fees trigger (highest-value, verbatim)
- "We could afford private for DS2... but with VAT and other probable tax rises, tutored him for the 11+ and now the state will pay our education bill and we save £30k a year." — VAT thread
- "I know a significant number of Y5/6 parents who are now targeting grammar schools instead of indies when they would not have done before." — VAT thread
