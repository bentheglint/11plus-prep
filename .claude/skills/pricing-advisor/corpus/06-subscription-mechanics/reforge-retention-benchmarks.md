## What this covers

Retention benchmarks are the empirical anchors that distinguish healthy subscription businesses from failing ones. Reforge, the product and growth education platform co-founded by Brian Balfour and Casey Winters, publishes frameworks for consumer subscription growth that incorporate data from RevenueCat's annual State of Subscription Apps reports. This file consolidates the most reliable published benchmarks for trial-to-paid conversion, plan-level retention, and category-specific performance — giving an 11+ app operator a calibrated baseline against which to measure its own metrics.

## The pattern / mechanic

**Trial-to-paid conversion:**

RevenueCat's 2024 State of Subscription Apps report (covering 30,000+ apps) found:

- Median trial-to-paid conversion: **37.3%** (down from 40.5% the prior year)
- App Store vs Google Play: **39.0%** vs **28.5%**
- Top quartile: approximately **44.9%**
- Bottom quartile: approximately **31.2%**
- Education category median trial start rate: **6.9%** of installs begin a trial

Trial duration matters significantly: 52% of all trials in 2024 were 5–9 days, and longer trials convert better. Education and Health & Fitness apps disproportionately use 5–9 day trials, reflecting the time needed for users to experience meaningful product value.

**Year-1 retention by plan type:**

| Plan type | Median Year-1 retention | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Annual | 35.3% first renewal | Down 13% YoY in 2024 |
| Monthly | 11.4% | Down 13.7% YoY in 2024 |
| Weekly | 3.4% | Structurally poor for most categories |

These numbers are cross-category medians. The top 5% of apps outperform the median by approximately 20x on revenue, suggesting the distribution is extremely right-skewed — a few excellent products capture most of the retention value.

**Geographic variance (annual plans):**

- Japan: 42% Year-1 retention
- North America: 27%
- Rest of World (including UK): 28%

UK apps should target the 28% median as a floor, not a goal. A well-executed edtech app with a structured curriculum and measurable outcomes should aspire to 35–40%+.

**Consumer subscription "good" vs "great" retention:**

The Subscription Value Loop framework (published via Reforge and Lenny Rachitsky's newsletter) frames retention quality in broader terms:

- Good consumer subscription retention: ~40% at 6 months
- Great consumer subscription retention: ~70% at 6 months
- Monthly churn of 5–10% is the consumer subscription norm; anything above 10% signals a structural retention problem

These 6-month benchmarks sit at a different timescale than RevenueCat's Year-1 annual data and are more useful for early-stage products where 12-month cohorts are not yet available.

**The 75% same-day trial start finding:**

A critical operational implication from the 2024 data: more than 75% of trial starts occur on the same day a user installs the app. This means retention is won or lost in the first session. An app that delivers a clear value signal in session one — a completed mini-test, a personalised score, a concrete improvement — will have materially better trial conversion than one that requires three sessions to reach its core value.

**Reactivation:**

RevenueCat's data shows 12% of churned monthly subscribers reactivate within 12 months, with some categories showing higher rates. ProfitWell data (Patrick Campbell) puts the 12-month reactivation figure at 20–30% for companies that execute price increases well. Both sources agree that churned users are not permanently lost — win-back campaigns have meaningful ROI.

**Benchmarks specifically for education apps:**

The 2024 RevenueCat data does not isolate 11+ or UK-specific education apps, but the education category as a whole shows:

- Above-average refund rates (suggesting expectation mismatch at purchase)
- Trial start rates of 6.9% of installs (below the all-category median)
- South Korea shows 10.7% download-to-paid conversion in education — an outlier driven by that market's extreme exam culture

The above-average refund rate in education is a warning signal: it suggests parents are purchasing based on marketing claims that the product then fails to substantiate quickly enough. This makes the first-session value demonstration even more critical.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app

A UK 11+ app targeting 35%+ Year-1 annual retention (above the Rest of World median of 28%) needs to win the first session decisively — the 75% same-day trial start data makes this the single highest-leverage retention intervention available. The above-average refund rate in education apps also suggests that an 11+ app should under-promise in its marketing and over-deliver on day one, rather than the reverse. Setting a target of 40% trial-to-paid conversion (above median) and 35%+ annual renewal is achievable if the product delivers a clear, personal value signal within the first 10 minutes of use.
