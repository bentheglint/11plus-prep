## Overview

Doodle Learning is a UK-based adaptive learning platform offering four core apps: DoodleMaths, DoodleTables, DoodleEnglish, and DoodleSpell. It targets children aged 4–14 (Reception through Year 9), with its primary sweet spot at primary age (Years 1–6). The platform uses its proprietary "Proxima" adaptive engine to personalise each child's daily work programme, targeting gaps rather than delivering a fixed curriculum sequence. It is well established in both the home and school markets, and is one of the more structurally comparable products to a UK 11+ prep app in terms of its family-subscription model and age range.

## Free vs paid

- All plans begin with a **free 7-day trial** — no payment taken upfront.
- After trial, full subscription pricing applies.
- There is no permanent free tier; the trial is the only free entry point.
- The 7-day trial is relatively short compared to competitors (Reading Eggs offers 30 days); this puts pressure on the onboarding experience to demonstrate value quickly.

## Family / sibling pricing

Doodle operates a clear two-tier structure: single-child and family (2–6 children). The family plan charges a fixed price regardless of whether 2 or 6 children use it, making it exceptional value for larger families. The jump from single to family for DoodleMaths is only £5/month (£12.99 vs £7.99), which is a low marginal cost per additional child and a strong conversion lever for parents with two or more children in the target age range.

## Schools licensing

Doodle has a substantial school market operation:

- **DoodleMaths + DoodleTables:** £6/pupil/year
- **DoodleEnglish + DoodleSpell:** £6/pupil/year
- **DoodleSuite (all 4 apps):** £9/pupil/year (reduced from £12)
- Multi-academy trust discounts available on request via dedicated account manager
- School accounts include a dedicated School Success Advisor and exportable tracking data

The school price (~£6–9/pupil/year) is dramatically lower than the parent-direct price (~£70–120/year per child). This is a standard B2B2C subsidy model — schools pay for access as a classroom tool, parents who want home practice pay full consumer rates.

## Unusual mechanics

- **Proxima adaptive engine:** Marketed as the core differentiator — the algorithm constructs each child's daily 10-minute session based on assessed gaps, not a fixed curriculum path.
- **Confidence score:** The app tracks and surfaces a child's "DoodleScore" (a confidence/mastery metric) to parents, which is a retention tool — parents want to see the score rise.
- **No gifting or lifetime plans:** Straightforward subscription-only model.
- **7-day free trial:** Shorter than most competitors; creates urgency but also limits the trial window for assessing genuine progress.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

Doodle's single/family two-tier structure at £7.99–£16.99/month is the closest structural parallel in the wider market to what a 11+ prep app would want to build. The family plan pricing (marginal cost of ~£5/month for additional children) is the benchmark to beat or match for multi-child households where siblings may both be preparing for selective school entry at different times.
