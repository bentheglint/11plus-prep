## Source

Atom Learning is the UK's leading 11+ exam prep digital platform for ages 7–11. Founded in London, it raised $25 million from SoftBank Vision Fund 2 in December 2021. Atom is a direct market competitor and its published data — outcomes reports, blog posts, pricing pages, and press mentions — provides the most directly applicable commercial benchmarks for an 11+ prep app considering its own pricing.

Key sources used:
- *91% of Atom Kids Get Offered Places at Top-Choice Schools* (Atom Learning blog, 2024)

- Press coverage: TechCrunch, Silicon Canals, The Telegraph, Yahoo News

## Key findings (the headline numbers)

**Platform scale:**
- Described as serving over **75,000 children** (figure cited on the platform and in investor coverage; date of this specific figure not confirmed in source material reviewed).
- Atom is described as being "loved by over 75,000 children" and is rated "Excellent" on Trustpilot.

**Outcomes data (2024 cohort):**
- **91% of Atom Home users received offers from their first-choice schools** in 2024. (Atom Learning blog, 2024)
- Placed children at: The Latymer School, Tiffin Schools, Aylesbury Grammar School, Chelmsford County High School for Girls (grammars); City of London School, Guildford High School, Forest School, Wellington College (independent schools).
- Many children were awarded academic scholarships.

**Market demand (post-VAT, 2024):**
- Atom reported a **39% jump** in parents preparing children for the 11-plus over the prior year following Labour's VAT-on-private-schools announcement. (The Telegraph, via Yahoo News, late 2024)
- **58%** of Atom parents now target grammar school placement; **28%** target private school admission. (Same source)

**Pricing (verified May 2026):**

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (÷12) | Annual total |
|---|---|---|---|
| EMS (English, Maths, Science) | £39.99 | £31.99/mo | £383.90 |
| Exam Prep | £59.99 | £47.99/mo | £575.90 |
| Exam Prep Plus | £69.99 | £55.99/mo | £671.90 |

- 20% saving for annual vs monthly billing across all plans.
- 5-day free trial on all plans.
- 20% sibling discount (second child while first subscription is active).
- Tuition (human tutors, separately): from **£54 per lesson**.
- GCSE tier also available: **£49.99/month** (Pro plan).

**Contextual tutoring cost data (from Atom's own blog):**
- National average 11+ tutor: **£55/hour** (weekly, 50 sessions = £2,750/year standard programme)
- Realistic all-in with materials: **£3,000–5,000/year**
- Extended programmes (2 years, multiple subjects): **£5,000–8,000**
- London/South East (London 1:1 one subject): **£90/hour** in 2025
- An unnamed Trustpilot reviewer reported that 4 weeks using Atom was "at least as valuable as his [child's] time with the tutor" over 9 months.
- **87% of Atom students** achieve admission to their first-choice school, "with the majority not using a private tutor." (Atom Learning marketing materials — note slight discrepancy with the 91% figure from the 2024 cohort report; 87% may be a historical or all-cohort average)

## Sample / methodology (if known)

Atom's outcomes data is self-reported based on their own user cohort. The 2024 figure (91%) is from Atom Home users who reported their school admission outcomes. No independent verification. As a commercial platform, Atom has strong incentive to report favourable outcomes. The pricing data is verified directly from their public pricing page.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

Atom Learning is the direct pricing anchor for any 11+ prep app. Their Exam Prep tier at **£59.99/month** (or £47.99/month annual) is the established market ceiling for a premium digital prep product. Their EMS tier at **£39.99/month** shows what parents will pay for core subjects without the full 11+ stack. The 5-day free trial and sibling discounts are standard conversion mechanisms in this market. Any new entrant pricing below £39.99/month has meaningful positioning room against Atom; pricing at £15–£25/month positions as accessible/challenger while being well below the £55/hour tutoring benchmark. The 39% demand surge post-VAT confirms the market is growing, not saturating.
