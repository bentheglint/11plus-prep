The kitchen-table problem

Practice books mark the answer. Nobody explains the question.

Most 11+ prep is a pile of past questions and an answer sheet. When your child gets one wrong, the book says which letter was right, and that's it. Someone still has to explain why, usually a parent, usually at the end of a long day.

That isn't a gap in your child, and it isn't a gap in you. It's a gap in the tools. The 11+ rewards children who have been taught the method behind each question type, not children who have simply seen the most questions.

PrepStep was built to close that gap: teach the method first, practise it properly, and explain every wrong answer in plain words.

The questions follow patterns

11+ questions fall into recurring types. Once a child is taught the pattern, it stops being frightening.

Every child has quiet weak spots

Strong at fractions, losing marks on ratio. The game is finding those spots early and turning them around.

Wrong answers are the goldmine

A mistake that gets explained, then practised again, becomes a mark gained in the real exam.

Questions parents ask

Straight answers, before you sign up.

Is it really free to start?

Yes. The free plan gives your child daily practice, with the same lessons and the same tutor, for as long as you like. We don't ask for card details, and there's no trial that quietly turns into a bill. You only pay if you choose to upgrade.

Which exam does PrepStep prepare for?

The 11+ for grammar schools in the Bournemouth and Poole area, covering Maths, English and Verbal Reasoning. Everything in PrepStep, from the lesson content to the mock test timing, is built around that exam.

Will the tutor just give my child the answers?

No, and that's deliberate. The tutor follows a no-spoiler rule: while your child is still working on a question it will nudge, hint and re-explain the method, but it won't reveal the answer. Once they've committed to an answer, it explains the full working in plain words.

How much time does it take each day?

Sessions are short by design: a brief lesson, then a focused quiz. Little and often beats a marathon Sunday, both for learning and for family peace. Mock tests are longer, and you choose when to run those.

Is my child's data safe?

Yes. PrepStep follows UK GDPR, shows no advertising, and never sells data. The tutor is built specifically for children, with guardrails to keep it focused on learning. Your child's data is used to run PrepStep for your child, nothing else.

What if it's not right for us?

Cancel any time from your account, no phone calls and no awkward forms. If you cancel a paid plan you keep access until the end of what you've paid for, and the free plan is always there.
