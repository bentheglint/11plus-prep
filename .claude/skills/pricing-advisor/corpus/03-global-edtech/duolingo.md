## Overview

Duolingo is the world's most downloaded language-learning app, with over 500 million registered users and roughly 40 million daily active learners as of 2025. Listed on the NYSE (DUOL), it operates a freemium model at global scale, offering courses in 40+ languages. Its primary market is adults learning independently, but it has a significant youth audience. Duolingo's business model is a textbook example of freemium done at scale: the free product is genuinely useful, and paid tiers remove friction rather than unlock core content. The app is available on iOS, Android, and web.

## Pricing tiers

**Free**
- Price: £0
- Billing: Ongoing
- Included: Full course content across all languages, leaderboards, streaks, limited hearts (lives), ads between lessons.

**Super Duolingo (formerly Duolingo Plus)**
- Price: $84/year (approx. £67/year); ~$6.99/month if billed monthly (approx. £5.60)
- Billing: Monthly or annual
- Included: Unlimited hearts (removes the mistake-gating mechanic), no ads, unlimited legendary challenges, streak repair, monthly streak freeze, progress quizzes, mastery quiz.

**Duolingo Max**
- Price: $168/year (approx. £134/year); ~$13.99/month if billed monthly (approx. £11.20)
- Billing: Monthly or annual
- Included: Everything in Super, plus two AI-powered features: Explain My Answer (GPT-4 explanations of why an answer was right or wrong) and Roleplay (open-ended conversation practice with an AI character). Max is currently available for Spanish and French learners on iOS only (limited rollout as of early 2026).

**Family Plan**
- Price: $120/year (approx. £96/year) for up to 6 accounts
- Billing: Annual only
- Included: Super Duolingo features for all 6 members; each member gets a separate account with independent progress. Managed by the plan owner.

## Free vs paid (the core question for global edtech)

Duolingo's free tier is intentionally substantive — all course content is free, forever. Monetisation is built around removing friction, not unlocking content. The primary friction mechanic is **hearts**: free users have a limited number of mistakes per day before they are locked out until hearts regenerate (or are replenished by watching ads or completing practice). This creates genuine pain for motivated learners, which is precisely the population most likely to pay.

The philosophy is explicit in Duolingo's investor communications: the free product must be good enough to attract massive top-of-funnel volume, because paid conversion is a numbers game at scale. Approximately 8–9% of active users pay (per analyst estimates), which at 40M DAU represents a meaningful revenue base. The company has stated it will never put core learning content behind a paywall.

## Family / multi-user pricing

The Family Plan at $120/year for 6 accounts (effective $20/person/year, approx. £16) is notably cheaper than 6 individual Super subscriptions ($84 × 6 = $504). This is a deliberate strategy to capture household-level commitment and reduce churn — once a family is on one plan, cancellation requires a household conversation rather than an individual impulse. The plan is billed annually only, which also improves revenue predictability. There is no per-child management dashboard for parents; each account is independent.

## Unusual mechanics

- **Heart-gating**: The single most-discussed aspect of Duolingo's monetisation. Free users are gated by mistakes, not time or content. This is psychologically potent because it penalises exactly the moment of learning — making an error — which drives upgrade intent.
- **Streak mechanic**: Streaks are central to Duolingo's retention engine. Super includes streak freeze and repair, which protects a streak investment that may span years. The fear of losing a long streak is a powerful conversion trigger.
- **Annual discount**: Monthly Super is ~$6.99; annual works out to $7/month — almost the same. The actual discount versus monthly is minimal, suggesting the annual push is more about cashflow and churn reduction than genuine price incentive.
- **Max tier A/B testing**: The Max tier is being slowly rolled out with AI features, with Duolingo using it to test willingness to pay for AI-native features before scaling.
- **Leaderboards**: Free users participate in competitive weekly leaderboards, which drives session frequency. Leaderboard position is visible to others, adding social pressure — a retention mechanic that costs Duolingo nothing.

## Positioning copy

Duolingo positions Super around freedom and flow: "Learn without limits." The emphasis is on removing the interruptions (ads, heart refill waits) rather than unlocking new content. Max is positioned as "the only app with AI conversation practice," leaning into the GPT-4 integration as a genuine capability differentiator. Family plan copy emphasises value: "Get Super for the whole family for less than the price of one individual plan."

## Lessons for a UK 11+ app at smaller scale

Duolingo's heart-gating mechanic is worth studying closely: it converts the learning act (making mistakes) into a monetisation trigger, which is clever but risky in a children's context where mistakes should be normalised. The family plan strategy — annual-only, heavily discounted per-seat — is directly applicable to a UK 11+ product where the purchasing unit is always the household. The core principle that all curriculum content should remain free, with paid tiers removing friction and adding insight (parent dashboards, progress data), is the right philosophy to carry across regardless of scale.
