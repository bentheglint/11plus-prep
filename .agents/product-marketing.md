## Product Overview
**One-liner:** 11+ prep that teaches before it tests.
**What it does:** PrepStep prepares children for the GL Assessment 11+ (Maths, English, Verbal Reasoning). Every focused session starts with a short, compulsory micro-lesson that teaches the method, then practises it, then a no-spoiler AI tutor explains anything the child gets wrong. It finds the few topics quietly costing marks and drills them until they stick, and gives parents a plain-English dashboard of exactly where their child is strong and weak.
**Product category:** 11+ exam-prep app (how parents search: "11+ practice", "GL Assessment practice", "11 plus app", "11+ tutor alternative").
**Product type:** B2C consumer subscription (web app, any device, nothing to download). Secondary B2B2C channel: tutors who refer families for 25% recurring commission.
**Business model:** Freemium subscription.
- **Free Forever:** £0, no card, no time limit. One 10-question quiz/day; streaks, progress, mistakes saved.
- **Full Access (PrepStep Plus):** £24.99/mo or £199/yr (= £16.58/mo, "save £101"). Unlimited practice, lesson before every focused quiz, AI tutor on every question, timed mock tests, full parent dashboard.
- **New-account trial:** everything unlocked free for 30 days, **no card required**, nothing auto-charged. Then drops to Free Forever unless they upgrade.
- **FSM / Pupil Premium:** full access free until exam day, on request.

## Target Audience

Honest local exception: Dorset grammars move GL→Quest for 2026 entry. **Planned market expansion:** once the question library is adapted for the **Quest Assessment**, PrepStep will also cover Quest regions (needs research to scope which regions and how the format differs).

**Primary buyer (the ICP):** A UK parent of a child in **Year 4 or Year 5** preparing for the GL Assessment 11+ for grammar-school or selective-independent entrance. Engaged, ambitious-for-their-child, cost-conscious relative to private tutoring, often anxious about a high-stakes one-shot exam.

**End user:** their child, **Year 4 or Year 5** (~9–10), sitting the exam at the start of Year 6.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** The 11+ rewards knowing a fixed set of recurring question patterns. Every child is strong on some and quietly dropping marks on others, usually before anyone has noticed which. Parents can't see where the marks are going, can't teach the method themselves, and can't afford (or sustain) a tutor for every gap.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Books/apps (Bond, CGP, Atom):** drill without pinpointing the weak spots or teaching a way through them; test cold; no one explains the wrong answers.
- **Private tutors:** excellent but expensive (£40–50/hr, £1,500+/year) and "gone by Tuesday" — one hour a week, no presence between lessons.
- **Free resources:** scattered, no tracking, no teaching, no diagnosis.
**What it costs them:** money (tutoring £1,500+/yr), time (parent trying to teach/diagnose), and the highest cost — the risk of the child missing the one-shot exam and the grammar-school place.
**Emotional tension:** anxiety over a single high-stakes exam; fear of wasting the one chance; guilt at not being able to help or afford more; overwhelm at not knowing where to focus.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators (what alternatives lack):**
- **Teach-before-test:** a short, *compulsory* micro-lesson before every focused quiz. No cold starts. (Named in the product: the "climb" — teach → practise → understand.)
- **No-spoiler AI tutor on every question:** nudges the child to the method while they work, only explains once they commit. "A tutor that sits on its hands."
- **Weak-spot detection:** tracks marks topic-by-topic, surfaces the exact few costing marks, and brings those questions back until secure.
- **GL-specific, not a generic reasoning quiz** repackaged for grammar entry. 8,000+ questions in real GL format (5-option MC, worked explanations, Foundation→Challenging).
- **Radically cheaper than the tutor it partially replaces**, and honest: no ads ever, no card to start, FSM free.
- **Honest, non-inflated progress.** Parents actively distrust tools that flatter (Atom's mock-vs-app score gap is a named complaint). Honesty here is a differentiator to lead with, not hide.
  > **⚠ ASPIRATIONAL / AT RISK — do NOT claim in copy yet.** Audit + Oracle benchmark (20 Jul): PrepStep's tests can flatter, but it is NOT the difficulty mix (our 30/40/30 matches GL). Real causes: (1) a **gameable answer-length tell** — English vocabulary has the correct answer as the longest option **46%** of the time (2.3× chance), so a child scores ~46% on vocab by picking the longest with zero knowledge; (2) likely **soft "difficulty 3" calibration**; (3) possible **item reuse** (mock = memory test). Fixes: Oracle-rewritten vocabulary distractors (neutralise length), empirical p-value audit on D1 data to catch soft D3, VR type-blocks, English 45 min + non-calculator. Until fixed, "honest, non-inflated progress" cannot be claimed in copy. Full diagnosis + prioritised fixes: `.agents/research/scoring-integrity-2026-07-20.md`.
- **Timing & technique, not just content.** Parents repeatedly say children fail by running out of time / guessing the last few, not by not knowing the material. Timed mock tests + method-first practice target the real gap.
**Why customers choose us:** the only tool that *teaches the method, finds the weak spots, and explains every wrong answer* — the three things a good tutor does — for less than one hour of tutoring a year.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "There's loads of free 11+ stuff online / I'll just buy the Bond books." | Free/books drill but don't teach the method, find the weak spots, or explain wrong answers. And you can start PrepStep free for 30 days, no card. |
| "Can an app really replace a tutor?" | It's not either/or. PrepStep does the teach-practise-explain a tutor does, every day, between the lessons a tutor can't cover, at ~1 hour of tutoring's yearly cost. |
| "Is it safe / is the AI safe for my child?" | UK GDPR compliant, no ads ever, and the tutor is built for children: stays on the lesson, won't wander off-topic or hand over answers. |
| "I have two children sitting it." | ⚠GAP — currently **one child per subscription** (multi-child is a parked FK rebuild). Handled ad hoc via email. A real objection until multi-child ships. |
| "Does it cover CEM / CET / Pre-Test?" | GL Assessment only today; CEM/Pre-Test on the 2027 roadmap. **Anti-fit for non-GL families right now.** |
| "£24.99/mo feels like a lot." | Anchor to the alternative: a tutor is £50–75/*hour* (parents' own quoted rates), PrepStep is £199 for the *whole year*, and there's a genuine free-forever tier. |
| "Isn't this too much pressure on my child?" | The register parents fear ("brutal", "stressed and upset"). Answer with calm: method-first, no cold testing, a tutor that coaches not crams, one calm step at a time. Never gamified hype. |
| "Plenty of kids do fine at state school / both mine failed and thrived." | Don't argue the choice. PrepStep is for families who've *decided* to sit the 11+ and want the calmest, most honest path through it. |
| "Is my child even a candidate?" | Parents self-assess with "top of the class", "greater depth". Meet them there: free to start, no card — try it and the dashboard shows you honestly where they stand. |

**Anti-persona:** families sitting **non-GL** exams (CEM, ISEB Pre-Test, region-specific formats) today; parents wanting a fully human/offline tutoring relationship; families needing multi-child on one plan right now.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** anxiety they can't see where marks are lost; tutoring too expensive/infrequent; books drill but don't teach; the exam looming.
**Pull:** teaches the method first; names the exact weak topics; a patient tutor on every wrong answer; free to try with no card; a fraction of tutoring cost.
**Habit:** already paying a tutor; already bought the Bond books; "we're managing"; inertia/loyalty to current tutor.
**Anxiety:** "Will an app actually work / is it as good as a person?"; "Is the AI safe?"; "Will I get charged?" (answered by no-card trial); "Is it right for our specific exam?" (GL-only).

## Brand Voice
**Tone:** warm, calm, plain, parent-to-parent. Reassuring, never hypey.
**Style:** direct and specific; short sentences; benefit-led; British English; honest to a fault (no fake testimonials, no ads, no card tricks). **No em dashes in consumer copy** (Ben's hard rule).
**Personality:** honest, warm, knowledgeable, calm, on-your-side. (Think: the good tutor who also happens to be a straight-talking parent.)
**VOC-critical tone note:** the audience's emotional register is **guilt and "the system is brutal/unfair", not excitement.** Copy that acknowledges the unfairness and offers the *calmest, most honest path through it* will outperform aspirational "get ahead" or gamified framing. Lead with empathy and diagnosis, not hype.
