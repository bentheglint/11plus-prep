## Source

Mumsnet Talk is the UK's largest parenting forum with millions of registered users. The secondary and education boards contain dozens of active threads in which parents share specific, real-world spend on 11+ tutoring. Three threads are the primary sources here:

- **"11+ Tuition £££ — How much did you spend?"** (thread 5201451, secondary board) — parents self-reporting total spend.
- **"Cost of 11plus tuition London"** (thread 4556907, secondary board) — London-specific hourly rates.
- **"£45 to £60 per hour for 11 plus tuition"** (thread 4880549, secondary board) — debate about whether high rates are justified.
- **"To ask how much are you paying for 11 plus tuition?"** (thread 5201637, AIBU board) — regional rate comparisons.

These threads are not a controlled study; they are self-selected parental disclosures. However, they provide ground-level data unavailable from formal research, including specific amounts, regional variation, and attitudes toward price.

## Key findings (the headline numbers)

**Total spend range across the threads:**
- Minimum observed (self-study only): **£6.99/month** for an online practice-test subscription (BOFA)
- Books-only approach: **£100–200 total** (single parent report)
- Weekly group tuition, one year: **~£1,100** (£30/session × 38 weeks, term-time only)
- Weekly 1:1, one year (standard): **~£1,500–2,000**
- Super-selective targeting, 18 months: **£6,500**

**Hourly rates by region:**
- North West (1:1): £30–£33/hour
- Kent (1:1): £25–£40/hour; group Zoom £14.50/hour
- Midlands (1:1): £30–£35/hour
- South West (1:1): £25–£27/hour
- South East non-London (1:1): £35–£50/hour
- London (SW): £60–£65/hour
- London (NW): £60–£75/hour
- London (Central, 1:1): £50–£100/hour
- London (recent 2025 report): £90 for 1:1 in a single subject; £60 for group

## Sample / methodology (if known)

Self-selecting community forum; no formal sampling. Contributors identify their region. Threads span 2022–2025. Data may skew toward more engaged, more affluent Mumsnet users relative to the general parent population. However, the platform has documented appeal across UK socioeconomic groups.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

Mumsnet data directly anchors the pricing context for an 11+ app. The £6.99/month BOFA reference establishes a floor: parents already pay for digital-only prep subscriptions at this level, and it is seen as reasonable. The data also reveals a strong split: parents in London and the South East are paying £60–£90/hour for tutors, making an app at £15–£40/month appear dramatically affordable by comparison. Parents who are price-sensitive (North West, Kent, rural) are still paying £25–£35/hour for 1:1, which means even monthly app costs near £20–£30 represent less than one session. The self-study market (books + practice tests only) shows willingness to spend £100–600 in total, suggesting a lower ceiling for users who resist tutoring — an app priced at £7–£15/month fits this segment.
