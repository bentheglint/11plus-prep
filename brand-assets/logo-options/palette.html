Accessibility check

WCAG 2.1 AA requires 4.5:1 for body text and 3:1 for UI components / large text. All combinations below pass.

Key rule: amber + pink are decorative only. When they appear as backgrounds, use dark ink text on top (not white).

Why these work for ages 9–11

Compared against Duolingo (Green/Orange/Blue/Yellow), Khan Kids (Teal/Coral/Yellow), Prodigy (Blue/Teal/Purple/Orange/Yellow), TTRS (Purple/Pink/Yellow/Teal) — the PrepStep palette sits in the same saturation range as every successful kids' platform.

Why 500-weight Tailwind values? They're deliberately calibrated to be saturated enough for energy while meeting WCAG. Industry convention for kids' apps.

What keeps it 9–11 and not younger? No primary-school rainbow (no simultaneous yellow/red/orange). No pastels. Colours are used with restraint — 1-2 per screen, not all at once. That's what distinguishes "ages 9-11 + parents" palettes from "ages 4-7" palettes.
