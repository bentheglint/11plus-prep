The quiet gap

The marks slip away quietly.

Most children are strong in some corners of the 11+ and quietly losing marks in others. The trouble is rarely effort. It is that a handful of question types keep catching them out, and a wrong answer on a worksheet often stays a mystery.

Nobody is there to explain why it was wrong, so the same gap turns up again the following week. Practice without understanding just rehearses the mistake.

PrepStep is built the other way round. It teaches the method first, then shows you exactly where the marks are going, and stays patient while your child works it out.

How it works

Three steps, in the right order.

The clue is in the name. Every focused session climbs the same three steps: teach the method, practise it where it matters, then understand every miss.

01Teach

A lesson comes first.

Every focused session opens with a short, compulsory lesson. Your child learns the method in plain words, before a single question is asked.

02Practise

Then they put it to work.

PrepStep finds where the marks are slipping, topic by topic, and brings those mistakes back to practise until they truly stick.

03Understand

The tutor explains the misses.

When something is tricky, a patient tutor steps in. It never hands over the answer. It nudges, waits, then explains once your child commits.

The tutor, in practice

It won’t just give the answer.

The built-in tutor follows one firm rule: no spoilers. While your child is still trying, it nudges them towards the answer rather than handing it over. Once they commit, it explains in plain words.

The question
A coat costs £48. In a sale it is reduced by 25%. What is the sale price?
Child tries, the tutor nudges, your child answers, then the tutor explains. That order is the whole point.
Child
I don’t get it. What’s the answer?
Tutor
I won’t give it away just yet. Start with this: what is 25% of £48?
Child
Um… £12?
Tutor
Exactly right. Now take that £12 off £48. What’s left?
Child
£36!
