## Source

UK national and specialist press coverage of private tutoring costs, 11+ exam prep spending, and the impact of VAT on private school fees (announced 2024, implemented January 2025). Direct BBC and Guardian fetches were blocked, so this file synthesises from: Yahoo News/The Telegraph coverage, Comprehensive Future (campaign organisation citing Guardian data), Atom Learning blog citing The Profs data, EdPlace commissioned research, and specialist outlets. All figures attributed where source is identifiable.

## Key findings (the headline numbers)

- **11-plus hourly rate (London, 2024–25):** Average cost reached **£91.25 per hour** between October 2023 and January 2024, up from £80/hour the previous year — a **14% year-on-year increase**. (The Profs tutoring agency, cited in Atom Learning blog and Yahoo/Telegraph coverage)
- **London one-to-one, 2025:** Up to **£90 per hour** for 1:1 in a single subject; group lessons £60/hour. (Mumsnet parent report, 2025; consistent with Atom Learning blog)
- **Standard annual programme cost (UK average):** One hour per week, ~50 sessions, at ~£55/hour = **approximately £2,750**. Realistic all-in range including materials and mock exams: **£3,000–5,000**. For two years or multiple subjects: **£5,000–8,000**. (Atom Learning, *How much does 11+ tutoring cost in 2026?*, 2026 — drawing on Tutorful/Tutorcruncher market data)
- **Atom Learning 39% growth data:** Atom Learning platform reported a **39% jump** in parents preparing children for the 11-plus over the past year following the VAT announcement. **58%** of Atom parents targeted grammar school placement; only **28%** targeted private school admission. (The Telegraph, cited via Yahoo News, late 2024)
- **Tutoring company demand surge:** British Teachers platform saw daily enquiries **double** (5–6 per day to 10 per day) after the private school VAT announcement. Titanium Tutors noted a "huge surge" in 11-plus coaching demand since September 2024. (Telegraph coverage, late 2024)
- **Independent schools raising fees:** 76% of independent schools surveyed said they would raise fees by more than 10% with VAT applied; average UK day-school fees of £16,656 annually projected to rise to approximately **£20,000**. (Press coverage of independent school sector response, 2024–25)
- **Grammar school tutoring advantage:** Around **70% of those who received tutoring** got into a grammar school, compared with **14% of those who did not**. (FFT Education Datalab research, cited in press coverage)
- **EdPlace market size estimate:** UK parents spend approximately **£6 billion per year** on private tutors; approximately **£2,750 per child per year** on average. (EdPlace, commissioned survey; note: EdPlace is an edtech provider so this figure may serve commercial purposes — the £2 billion Sutton Trust figure is more conservative and peer-reviewed)

## Sample / methodology (if known)

- The Profs hourly rate data: drawn from Atom Learning blog and corroborated by Mumsnet reports for the same period.
- EdPlace figure of £6 billion: from a commissioned survey; methodology not disclosed. The Sutton Trust cites £2 billion — the higher figure likely includes all tutor spend including A-level and degree-level.
- Telegraph/Yahoo coverage: journalistic reporting drawing on tutoring agency self-reports (inherently interested parties).
- Grammar school tutoring advantage stat: FFT Education Datalab, a credible independent analytics provider.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

The press data from 2023–25 shows two things clearly: (1) tutoring costs are rising fast — up 14% year-on-year in London — widening the price gap between private tutoring and app-based alternatives; (2) VAT on private school fees has structurally redirected demand toward grammar school prep, specifically 11+ tutoring. Both trends are commercially favourable for an 11+ prep app. The 39% demand surge Atom Learning reported after the VAT announcement suggests timing-sensitive pricing and marketing opportunities. Positioning an app at £40–£70/month as a transparent, affordable alternative to £90/hour tutoring is both accurate and resonant.
