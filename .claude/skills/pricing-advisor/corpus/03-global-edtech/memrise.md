## Overview

Memrise is a UK-founded (London, 2010) language learning platform with over 65 million registered users. It was built on spaced repetition and user-generated memory techniques ("mems"), but has evolved significantly — particularly after being acquired and then spun off — into a platform emphasising real-world video content and, most recently, AI conversation practice. Memrise occupies an interesting middle position: it has a genuine free tier, a single paid tier, and a lifetime option that is frequently promoted at heavy discount. It is notable for its "Learn with Locals" video content (short clips of native speakers using target language in natural contexts) and for being one of the earlier language apps to introduce AI conversation features as a paid differentiator. Memrise is primarily a direct-to-consumer product with no meaningful B2B or school channel.

## Pricing tiers

**Free**
- Price: £0 / $0
- Billing: Ongoing
- Included: Access to core vocabulary learning and spaced repetition for all languages, basic grammar points, some video content (limited), community courses. The free tier is more restricted than Duolingo's but more substantial than Babbel's or Brilliant's demo tiers.

**Pro (Monthly)**
- Price: $39.99/month (approx. £32/month) — this is the anchor/headline monthly price
- Billing: Monthly, cancel anytime
- Included: Full access to all Memrise content, all "Learn with Locals" videos, AI conversation partner, offline access, leaderboards, progress stats, grammar deep dives, listening skills exercises.
- Note: This monthly price is aggressively high as an anchor — it is not the price most users pay.

**Pro (Annual)**
- Price: $89.99/year (approx. £72/year), equating to ~$7.50/month
- Billing: Annual upfront
- Included: Everything in monthly Pro. Annual discount is approximately 81% off the monthly anchor price — one of the most extreme monthly-to-annual price gaps in consumer edtech.

**Lifetime**
- Price: $199 standard; frequently promoted at $99 or lower (approx. £160 standard, approx. £79 on promotion)
- Billing: One-time payment
- Included: Permanent access to all current and future Memrise content. Lifetime access is a recurring promotional offer rather than a consistently available standard tier — Memrise uses "lifetime sales" periodically to capture high-intent users.

## Free vs paid (the core question for global edtech)

Memrise uses a partial-content free tier that gives users enough to assess the product but restricts the most compelling features — particularly the video content and AI conversation — behind the Pro paywall. This is a middle-ground approach: more generous than Babbel's one-lesson demo, less generous than Duolingo's full-content free tier.

The AI conversation feature is explicitly Pro-only and is the primary current conversion argument for users who are on the fence. This positions AI not as a general product feature but as a premium capability — a framing that could be directly relevant to any edtech product considering AI-assisted learning features.

The extreme anchor pricing ($39.99/month) is a deliberate psychological technique: users who see the monthly price first regard the annual price ($7.50/month effective) as a rescue, and the lifetime price as an obvious no-brainer. The real product is the annual subscription; the monthly price exists primarily to make everything else look cheap.

## Family / multi-user pricing

Memrise does not offer a family plan. Each user requires an individual subscription. This is a structural gap for a product used by students, where parents might purchase for multiple children. There is no multi-seat discount. A household with two learners pays two full Pro subscriptions — at annual pricing, $179.98/year (approx. £144/year) combined. The absence of a family plan is likely a product prioritisation decision given Memrise's adult-learner focus rather than a deliberate commercial choice.

## Unusual mechanics

- **Extreme anchor pricing**: The $39.99/month anchor is one of the most aggressive in consumer edtech — approximately 4x higher than the effective annual price. Its purpose is purely psychological: to make the annual plan feel like an extraordinary deal. Very few users ever pay the monthly rate; those who do are typically on short-term plans with urgent goals.
- **Lifetime as periodic promotion**: Rather than offering lifetime access at a fixed price year-round, Memrise uses limited-time lifetime sales (often around Black Friday, January, and other promotional windows) to create urgency. The standard lifetime price ($199) is rarely paid; $99 or below is the effective promotional price. This creates a pattern where engaged users wait for the next sale rather than purchasing at full price — a risk for any product using this mechanic.
- **"Learn with Locals" video content**: Short, informal video clips of native speakers are both a product differentiator and a content production investment. Unlike Duolingo's AI-generated content, Memrise's video library requires ongoing production resources, which may be a constraint on the platform's ability to add new languages rapidly.
- **AI conversation as Pro differentiator**: Memrise's AI conversation partner (which simulates real-world dialogue scenarios) is explicitly a paid feature. This is one of the clearest examples in consumer edtech of AI capability being used as a tier-upgrade driver.
- **Spaced repetition roots**: The original Memrise "mems" system (user-created memory images and mnemonics) is available in modified form in the free tier, creating community-generated content that reduces Memrise's content production costs for vocabulary.

## Positioning copy

Memrise's main positioning is real-world language: "Learn languages the way they're actually spoken." The "Learn with Locals" video content is central to the brand: "Real people. Real conversations. Real results." Pro upgrade copy leads with the AI conversation feature: "Have real conversations with an AI, day or night." Lifetime promotion copy uses urgency and value framing: "Never pay again — lifetime access at [X]% off." The anchor-price technique is visible in all pricing page layouts: monthly price is always shown first, most prominently, before being "beaten" by annual and lifetime options.

## Lessons for a UK 11+ app at smaller scale

Memrise's extreme monthly anchor ($39.99 vs $7.50/month annual effective) is a pricing psychology lesson worth studying but deploying carefully — it works for Memrise because most users come via promotions or recommendations and never see the monthly price as a genuine option. For a 11+ app, a more moderate anchor (perhaps 2–3x the annual monthly equivalent) would achieve the same psychological effect without risking trust damage if users notice the disparity. The AI-as-premium-differentiator strategy is highly relevant: positioning AI-assisted feedback or AI conversation as a paid upgrade is a commercially sound approach that also gives the free tier a clear, compelling upgrade path. The lifetime sale model is useful for launch periods — offer lifetime access during a launch window to capture early adopters and generate word-of-mouth, then move to standard subscription pricing.
