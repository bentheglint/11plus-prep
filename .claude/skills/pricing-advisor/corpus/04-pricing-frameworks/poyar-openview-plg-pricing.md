## Who / what

Kyle Poyar is a partner at OpenView Partners, the expansion-stage venture capital firm that coined the term "product-led growth" (PLG) and has been its most rigorous documenter. Poyar runs OpenView's *Growth Unhinged* newsletter and is lead author of the annual OpenView Product Benchmarks report, which surveys 450+ software companies on pricing, conversion, and growth metrics. He is the person most cited when founders ask "what's a normal freemium conversion rate?" — because OpenView's data is one of the few sources with enough companies to produce meaningful benchmarks rather than anecdote.

## Core ideas (their key beliefs / frameworks)

- **Product-led growth replaces the traditional sales funnel with a five-stage user journey**: Discover → Start → Activate → Convert → Scale. Pricing decisions should be designed around moving users through each transition, not around maximising the initial transaction.
- **55% of software companies now identify as product-led** (up from 45% in 2019), but finding success is growing harder as the motion matures and competition intensifies.
- **Freemium vs. free trial is a real strategic choice**, not a preference:
  - *Choose freemium* if your product has network effects, you want to maximise top-of-funnel volume, or your sales team is lean and conversion can happen asynchronously.
  - *Choose free trial* if you want higher near-term conversion and can support a sales-assist motion.
  - *Consider reverse trials* (start everyone on full-featured paid experience, then downgrade non-converters to free) as a hybrid that captures the benefits of both — Poyar believes most products with strong UX should attempt this.
- **Free-to-paid conversion benchmarks** (from the 2022 Product Benchmarks survey, 450+ companies):
  - Freemium self-serve: 3–5% is good, 6–8% is great.
  - Freemium with sales-assist: 5–7% is good, 10–15% is great.
  - Free trial: 8–12% is good, 15–25% is great.
  - Developer-focused products convert at approximately half the rate of non-developer products (median ~5% vs ~10%).
  - One-third of freemium products fall in the 2.5–5% range; 20% see below 2.5%.
- **The 1,000-visitor math**: a freemium PLG product attracting 1,000 daily unique visitors can expect ~60 free sign-ups (6% visit-to-signup) and ~3–4 paying customers (5% free-to-paid). This is the baseline to beat.
- **Conversion rates double with sales touchpoints**: companies with 11+ touchpoints in the free-to-paid journey perform 2× better than those with fewer. But freemium converts 25% more efficiently without sales assistance when it does convert.
- **Product-qualified accounts (PQAs) > product-qualified leads (PQLs)**: the unit of sales motion has shifted from individual users showing engagement signals to accounts showing collective purchasing potential. Poyar advocates a scoring continuum across engagement, seniority, and feature usage depth rather than a binary PQL flag.
- **Expansion revenue is the PLG endgame**: in a healthy PLG model, new customer revenue is a minority of total ARR growth. Seat expansion, feature upsells, and usage overages compound. Pricing must be designed to enable this from day one.

## Method / how to apply

**Freemium vs. free trial decision framework (Poyar)**

| Criterion | Points to freemium | Points to free trial |
|---|---|---|
| Network effects exist | Y | |
| Sales team is small or self-serve | Y | |
| Conversion can happen async | Y | |
| Need near-term revenue conversion | | Y |
| Can run sales-assist on free signups | | Y |
| UX supports full trial without friction | | Y |

If neither direction is clear, run a reverse trial: give all new users full-feature access for a defined period (e.g. 14 days), then gate at conversion.

**The new PLG funnel (five stages)**
1. *Discover* — generate high-intent traffic at low CAC (SEO, community, product virality).
2. *Start* — convert visitors to free signups. Benchmark: ~6% of unique visitors.
3. *Activate* — reduce time-to-value. Define your "aha moment" and instrument the path to it.
4. *Convert* — trigger upgrade at the moment of maximum value realisation. Benchmark: 3–8% of free users within 6 months.
5. *Scale* — expand revenue through additional seats, features, or usage.

**ICP identification (keeping it simple)**
Poyar recommends starting with five signals before over-engineering lead scoring:
1. Business email?
2. Domain lookup via data enrichment tool.
3. Right seniority level?
4. Right company size and industry?
5. Target geography?

Tighten the ICP as sales capacity limits become real, not before.

## Applicability to a UK 11+ app at pre-launch / early-launch stage

Poyar's benchmarks give a 11+ app founder a realistic expectation: if you launch freemium without sales-assist, expect 3–5% of free accounts to convert to paid within six months under normal conditions. For a UK consumer app targeting parents — not enterprise buyers — the self-serve freemium path is the only realistic option, and 5% should be the minimum conversion target to make the economics work. His reverse trial concept is particularly worth testing: parents who experience a full-featured 14-day run before hitting a paywall will have a much clearer signal of value than those who hit limitations immediately. The PLG funnel model also implies that an 11+ app should design its "aha moment" (the instant the child gets their first score, completes their first topic, or the parent sees a progress report) into the onboarding flow as the primary conversion trigger.
