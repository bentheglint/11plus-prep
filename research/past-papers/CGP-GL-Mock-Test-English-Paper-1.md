## Section 1 — Comprehension

### Passage: "The Fiend of the Fens" (Fiction)

**Genre:** Third-person narrative fiction  
**Setting:** The Fens of England, approximately 60 years ago  
**Characters:** Norman and Neville (two young brothers), William (their father, a gamekeeper), their mother

**Passage summary:**
About 60 years ago, stories circulated in the flatlands of England about a mysterious beast roaming the water-filled dykes and fields of the Fens. Rumours grew, and newspapers reported sightings of "The Fiend of the Fens". Norman and Neville, the gamekeeper's sons, decide to dig pits to trap the beast. Despite having little materially, they have fertile imaginations. Their father William feeds their excitement with tales of strange disappearances and shadowy sightings. After digging a first pit and covering it with camouflage (sticks, sacks, grass, leaves), they discover their father has tricked them by falling into it. They then dig a second pit at the front of the cottage. The story climaxes when a figure disappears into the pit like a bucket dropped down a well.

**Key passage lines (as referenced in questions):**

## Potential Coverage Gaps — For Oracle Review

1. **Negative retrieval** (Q1, Q17, Q18) — "which was NOT" / "which is FALSE" — a distinct comprehension skill that catches children who misread the question. Worth checking if the app explicitly practises this.

2. **Simile vs metaphor distinction** (Q25) — the passage contains both figurative language types; children must distinguish simile specifically.

3. **Collective nouns** (Q26) — "pair", "flock", "swarm" etc. Worth checking app grammar coverage depth.

4. **Relative pronouns** (Q47) — "which", "who", "whom", "whose", "that" — a cloze staple that tests precise grammar knowledge.

5. **Third conditional** (Q49) — "If I had eaten" — advanced grammar structure. May be beyond core 11+ scope but appeared here.

6. **Connectives for contrast vs consequence** (Q46) — "However" vs "Therefore" vs "Consequently" — a cloze staple.

7. **Spelling patterns in this paper:** The 8 spelling errors test: -ing doubling rule (beginning), -ite/-itely (definitely), -ous/-iously (noticeably), -ate/-ately (desperately), vowel confusion (believed). Worth cross-referencing against app spelling sub-concepts.

8. **Punctuation: apostrophes in two contexts** (Q35: plurals; Q41: possessives) — apostrophe misuse is the dominant error type in Q35–42.
