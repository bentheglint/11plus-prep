## Overview

Busuu is a UK-founded (London, 2008) language learning platform with approximately 120 million registered users, owned by Chegg since 2022. It offers courses in 13 languages across iOS, Android, and web. Busuu occupies a middle-market position between Duolingo's gamified free tier and Babbel's premium-only positioning: it has a genuine (if limited) free product, a single paid Premium tier, and a notable differentiator in community-based correction, where learners review each other's writing and speaking exercises. Busuu also issues McGill University-backed certificates of completion, which gives its premium tier a credential angle rare in consumer language apps.

## Pricing tiers

**Free**
- Price: £0 / $0
- Billing: Ongoing
- Included: Access to 2 out of 14 units per course, community correction submissions (limited), basic vocabulary exercises. Noticeably more restricted than Duolingo's free tier.

**Premium (Monthly)**
- Price: ~£8.99/month (UK) / ~$9.99/month (US)
- Billing: Monthly, cancel anytime
- Included: Full access to all course units, unlimited community corrections, offline access, grammar exercises, personalised study plan, vocabulary review with spaced repetition, McGill University certificate of completion, ad-free.

**Premium (Annual)**
- Price: ~£3.92/month billed annually (~£47/year) (UK) / ~$3.99/month billed annually (~$47.88/year) (US)
- Billing: Annual upfront
- Included: Everything in monthly Premium. Annual discount approximately 56% off monthly — one of the steepest in the sector.

**Business / Teams**
- Price: Custom pricing per seat (minimum team sizes apply)
- Included: All Premium content, team management, reporting dashboard, dedicated account manager for larger teams.

**Family Plan**
- Busuu has tested family-tier options in some markets; availability in UK is inconsistent as of early 2026. Where available, typically 2 accounts at a modest discount.

## Free vs paid (the core question for global edtech)

Busuu's free tier is more restricted than Duolingo's — only 2 of 14 units per course are accessible — making it closer to an extended trial than a genuine ongoing product. This drives higher paid conversion rates (estimated 5–10% of active users) at the cost of a smaller engaged free base. The philosophy seems to be that partial content access creates stronger pull-to-upgrade than friction mechanics like hearts, because users can see exactly what they're missing.

Community correction is available in both free and paid tiers, but free users have submission limits. This mechanic is unusual in edtech: it creates a user-generated-content flywheel where paid users' corrections generate value for the platform, reducing the cost of human-reviewed feedback.

## Family / multi-user pricing

Busuu's family offering is inconsistent across markets and not a primary part of its go-to-market strategy. Where a family plan exists, it typically covers 2 accounts at a combined price slightly below two individual premium subscriptions. This is a weaker family proposition than Duolingo (6 accounts) or even Brilliant's corporate plan. For a household with multiple learners, Busuu is expensive relative to the family-friendly alternatives.

## Unusual mechanics

- **McGill University certificate**: Busuu is the only major consumer language app to offer a university-backed certificate of completion (in partnership with McGill University, Montreal). This adds a credential layer to what is otherwise a personal-use subscription, and functions as a conversion argument for learners with professional or academic motivations.
- **56% annual discount**: Like Brilliant, Busuu uses a very steep monthly-to-annual discount (56% off monthly) to anchor the monthly price high and make annual commitment appear essential. The monthly price (~£8.99) is positioned as the "expensive option" users pay for flexibility.
- **Community correction mechanic**: Native speakers correct written and spoken submissions from learners. This is a genuine product differentiator that also creates community stickiness — learners build relationships with regular correctors. No direct competitor has a comparable mechanic at scale.
- **Spaced repetition**: Premium includes an algorithmic vocabulary review system, which is a standard feature in language apps but is presented as a premium benefit by Busuu.
- **Chegg ownership integration**: Since the Chegg acquisition, Busuu has been integrated into Chegg's study platform ecosystem. This has limited visible impact on UK consumer pricing as of early 2026.

## Positioning copy

Busuu leads with speed and outcomes: "Join 120 million learners" and "Learn a language in just 10 minutes a day." The Premium upgrade page emphasises certificate value and community: "Get certified by McGill University" and "Get corrections from native speakers." Annual plan CTA typically reads "Best Value" alongside the per-month price broken out. The community correction feature is marketed as "real human feedback" in contrast to purely algorithmic app-based learning.

## Lessons for a UK 11+ app at smaller scale

The community correction mechanic — where users review each other's work — has an interesting analogue in a 11+ context: peer review of practice essays or verbal reasoning explanations could create community stickiness at low infrastructure cost. The McGill certificate strategy is directly transferable: a 11+ app could partner with a credible third party (a tutoring association, an educational charity) to issue a "course completion" credential that parents value. The steep annual discount (56%) combined with a restricted free tier is a coherent strategy worth modelling for a UK app where the exam deadline creates a natural annual purchase window.
