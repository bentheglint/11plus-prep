## Overview

Bond is the UK's most recognised 11+ preparation brand — its assessment papers have been the industry standard for decades. The online offering has quietly evolved into a three-tier product spanning an unusually wide price range: a legacy Bond-native subscription at the budget end, and two AI-powered tiers delivered via a partnership with Century Tech (an adaptive learning platform) at mid-market and premium prices. This creates a rare situation in which a single brand simultaneously occupies the budget, mid-market, and premium positions. The core audience is grammar school-focused families with children aged 9–11, with the top tier also targeting independent school entrance (ISEB Pre-Tests).

## Free vs paid

- **Bond-native tier:** No free trial confirmed on the product page; sign-up is direct to purchase.
- **Century Tech tiers (Premium and Premium Plus):** Both include a **7-day free trial** with no commitment required. This is the main free-entry point for the premium range.

## Family / sibling pricing

None observed across any tier. Multi-child families pay full price per child.

## Unusual mechanics

- **Three-brand architecture:** Bond's legacy subscription (Bond-built), and two tiers running on Century Tech's AI platform — all sold under the Bond brand. Parents encounter a seamless brand experience, but the underlying technology changes completely between the basic and premium tiers.
- **Annual billing framed as monthly rate:** Both Century Tech tiers display the per-month equivalent of the annual price, making the discount feel concrete rather than abstract.
- **ISEB endorsement at top tier:** Premium Plus carries ISEB-endorsed content, giving it a formal accreditation that competitors cannot easily replicate.
- **No lifetime, gifting, or pause options** observed.

## Positioning copy

- "Supercharge your child's 11+ learning" — Century Tech / Bond landing page headline.
- "Cutting-edge online learning for the 11+ exam created by Bond and CENTURY" — emphasises the co-brand and technology credibility.
- The platform cites independent research showing "children using Bond materials achieve greater improvements in test scores" vs alternatives.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

Bond's three-tier span (£5.42/month → £20.66/month → £28.99/month annually) maps the entire mid-market with a single brand. The strategic lesson: the brand does the heavy lifting, so pricing can stretch wide. A new entrant without brand equity needs to pick one tier to win rather than attempting to span the full range. Bond's mid-tier (£20–31/month) is the most exposed — parents may question whether the Century Tech AI layer adds enough over the basic Bond subscription to justify a 4× price jump.
