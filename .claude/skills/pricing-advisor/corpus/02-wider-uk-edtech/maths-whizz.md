## Overview

Maths-Whizz (published by Whizz Education) is an AI-powered virtual maths tutor for children aged 5–13. It delivers personalised maths lessons through an adaptive engine that assesses a child's "Maths Age" on first use and then generates a tailored learning path. The platform tracks a measurable "Maths Age" score that advances as the child progresses, which serves as both a learning metric and a retention/engagement tool. Maths-Whizz is one of the most premium-priced products in the UK home edtech market and positions explicitly as an alternative to private tuition. It is used in both homes and schools across 150+ countries.

## Pricing tiers

| Plan | First child | Additional children | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly | £19.99/month | £14.99/month each | 7-day free trial included |
| Annual | £149.99/year | £99.99/year each | Equivalent to £12.50/month; 7-day free trial included |

The annual plan represents a 38% saving over monthly for the first child (£149.99 vs £239.88 monthly equivalent). Additional children on annual plans save 33% over their monthly equivalent.

## Free vs paid

- **7-day free trial** on all plans; full access during the trial period.
- Cancellation during the trial incurs no charge.
- No permanent free tier — all content is paywalled after the trial.
- The 7-day trial is notably short relative to the premium price point; it is designed to allow a first assessment session and 1–2 learning sessions, rather than a full product evaluation.

## Family / sibling pricing

Maths-Whizz uses a per-child add-on model rather than a flat family bundle. Each additional child is priced at £14.99/month or £99.99/year on top of the first-child subscription. This is a transparent but potentially expensive model for large families:

- 2 children, annual: £149.99 + £99.99 = **£249.98/year**
- 3 children, annual: £149.99 + £99.99 + £99.99 = **£349.97/year**

This compares unfavourably with flat-rate family plans (e.g. Reading Eggs at £79.99 for 4 children). However, Maths-Whizz's positioning as a premium tutoring substitute justifies the per-child model — parents are conceptually paying per child, not per household.

## Schools licensing (if applicable)

Whizz Education has a substantial school and district-level sales operation, though school pricing is not published on the consumer website. Schools use a different product tier with teacher reporting, curriculum mapping, and class management tools. The company operates in over 150 countries and has significant B2B revenue, though the consumer product reviewed here is the family-facing offering.

## Unusual mechanics

- **Performance money-back guarantee:** Maths-Whizz guarantees that a child's Maths Age will improve by 12 months or more within one year of use (based on 60 minutes of usage per week). If this is not achieved, a full refund is offered. This is one of the most specific outcome guarantees in UK edtech and is a strong conversion tool for risk-averse parents.
- **Maths Age metric:** The proprietary "Maths Age" score (e.g. "Maths Age 8 years 4 months") is shown to parents and used in marketing. It creates a concrete, legible measure of progress that parents can track and share.
- **"Less than £3 per hour" framing:** Marketing copy positions the annual price as equivalent to approximately £3/hour of learning, comparing favourably to private tutors at £30–60/hour.
- **AI-powered virtual tutor framing:** Positions the product as a digital replacement for human tutoring rather than as a subscription app — a premium positioning choice that justifies the price.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

Maths-Whizz demonstrates that UK parents will pay a premium price (£150/year) for a maths product when backed by a concrete outcome guarantee and a "tutor equivalent" positioning. A UK 11+ app targeting selective school preparation — with arguably higher parental stakes than general maths improvement — could credibly adopt similar outcome-guarantee mechanics and tutor-alternative framing to justify premium pricing.
