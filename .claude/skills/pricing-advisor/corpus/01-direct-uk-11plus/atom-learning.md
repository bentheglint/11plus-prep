## Overview

Atom Learning is the dominant premium player in the direct UK 11+ market. It targets children aged 7–11 preparing for grammar and independent school entrance, and pitches itself explicitly against private tutors — positioning its monthly subscription as a fraction of the cost of a weekly tutoring session. The platform delivers personalised, adaptive practice across English, maths, verbal reasoning, and non-verbal reasoning, with a structured week-by-week learning plan at higher tiers. Atom's headline credibility claim — "87% of Atom students received an offer from their top choice school" — does a great deal of conversion work. It is the benchmark that any new entrant will be compared against by informed parents.

## Pricing tiers

| Tier | Monthly price | Annual price (billed yearly) | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|
| EMS | £39.99/month | £31.99/month (£383.90/year) | Daily personalised practice — English, maths, science. No mock tests. |
| Exam Prep | £59.99/month | £47.99/month (£575.90/year) | Structured learning plan covering all 11+ subjects inc. VR and NVR. Week-by-week progression. No mock tests. |
| Exam Prep Plus | £69.99/month | £55.99/month (£671.90/year) | Everything in Exam Prep plus unlimited online and printable mock tests with instant marking and detailed feedback. |

Annual billing saves approximately 20% across all tiers.

## Free vs paid

Atom offers a **5-day free trial** with no credit card required at sign-up. This is a low-friction entry point designed to let parents and children experience the platform before committing. No meaningful free tier exists after the trial ends — all substantive practice is behind the paywall.

## Family / sibling pricing

None observed. Each child requires a separate subscription at full price. This is a notable gap given that many 11+ families have two children preparing simultaneously, or have a younger child coming up behind.

## Unusual mechanics

- **No credit card at trial start** — reduces sign-up friction significantly and is called out explicitly in marketing.
- **Annual billing at a fixed monthly rate** — Atom displays the monthly equivalent (e.g. "£31.99/month") rather than the annual lump sum, which makes the saving feel more tangible.
- **Printable mock tests** at the top tier — unusual in a digital-first product and likely responsive to parent demand for paper-based exam simulation.
- No gift subscriptions, lifetime plans, or pause functionality observed.

## Positioning copy

- "87% of Atom students received an offer from their top choice school."
- Atom positions its pricing implicitly against private tutors: at £59.99/month, the product costs less than a single hour of tutoring per week — a comparison well-understood by the target audience.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

Atom establishes the premium ceiling: parents are willing to pay £60–70/month for a credible, well-branded adaptive platform. Any new entrant priced above Atom needs a compelling differentiation story; priced below Atom (£20–40/month), it can position as the smart, accessible alternative without needing to match Atom's feature depth on day one.
