## 1. Atom Learning (Market Leader)

**URL:** atomlearning.com | **Pricing:** GBP 39.99-69.99/month | **5-day free trial**

Atom is the dominant platform for 11+ preparation in the UK. Their analytics are the most mature and the benchmark to beat.

### Metrics Shown

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Performance Levels** | Four tiers: "Needs Practice", "Good", "Strong", "Master" |
| **Mastery Score** | 0-4000 per subject, with badge indicator |
| **Standardised Age Score (SAS)** | Calculated per mock test, same method as real exams. 100 = average; 115+ = selective school ready; 120+ = competitive grammar; 130+ = top 4%; 140+ = top 1% |
| **Target School Score** | Compares child's performance to other Atom users who applied to the same school (up to 5 target schools) |
| **Subtopic Breakdown** | Probability of answering an average-difficulty question correctly per subtopic |
| **Time Per Question** | Shown in mock test transcripts |
| **Weekly Activity** | Tasks completed, time spent |

### Visualisations

- **Track Page** — subject-by-subject progress bars showing strengths and areas for improvement
- **Topic-level heat maps** — maths performance by topic (colour-coded)
- **Performance graph by subtopic** — per mock test, showing probability of correct answer vs year group average
- **Target school comparison** — bar/line showing child's position relative to successful applicants
- **Learning trends over time** — week-to-week improvement tracking
- **Mock test transcripts** — clickable, per-question detail with time taken

### Exam Readiness Indicator

**Yes, strong.** The SAS system is the core readiness indicator. Parents can see:
- Their child's SAS after each mock test
- How it compares to the average SAS of children who chose the same target school
- Clear benchmarks: 115+ for selective, 120+ for competitive grammar
- Atom claims 91% of their students get offers from top-choice schools

Teachers were informally told to add 7-10 marks to Atom SAS scores for a "real life" equivalent, suggesting Atom scores slightly underpredict actual exam performance.

### Unique Features

- **Password-protected Track page** — parents can hide SAS from children who might find scores demotivating
- **Personalised weekly learning plan** — AI algorithm uses thousands of data points to schedule activities, auto-adjusting for weak areas
- **Paper test upload** — photograph a paper test, upload to Atom, get instant SAS and analysis
- **Peer benchmarking** — the only platform that compares against children applying to the same specific school
- **15-minute refresh** — performance levels update every 15 minutes
- **Mastery scores use last 30 questions** — recalculated nightly per topic/skill

### Parent Feedback (Mumsnet, Trustpilot)

**Praise:**
- "The analysis of a child's performance, strong and weak areas, and being able to select practice based on this is phenomenal"
- "Easy to see where my child is at a glance and to set extra questions"
- Multiple parents say Atom's tracking is far ahead of Bond
- 89% first-choice offers reported for 2025

**Complaints:**
- Dashboard format changed (new "target school" view replaced "average atom user" without clear explanation)
- Mastery score sometimes doesn't change despite practice (confused parents)
- Some NVR questions have multiple correct answers
- Interface can be "clunky" for finding specific features
- SAS accuracy questioned — some parents report significant differences vs real exam scores

## 4. IXL

**URL:** ixl.com | **Not 11+ specific, but best-in-class analytics**

IXL is a US-based maths and English platform used in UK schools. While not targeting 11+ specifically, their analytics system is the gold standard for educational progress tracking.

### Metrics Shown — Six Parent Reports

| Report | What It Shows |
|--------|---------------|
| **Usage Details** | Questions answered, time spent, skill progress, practice by category and day, per-session breakdown |
| **Trouble Spots** | Skills needing support, grouped by scaffolding level — instantly shows problem areas |
| **Score Chart** | Per-subject SmartScore for every skill practised, plus questions answered and time spent |
| **Questions Log** | Every question answered for each skill — full audit trail for coaching |
| **Progress & Improvement** | Visual graph of SmartScore growth over time |
| **Student Summary** | Comprehensive overview with Assessment Levels graph, accomplishments, and specific next-step recommendations |

### SmartScore System

IXL's proprietary scoring algorithm (not a percentage) that accounts for:
- Questions answered correctly/incorrectly
- Question difficulty level
- Recency of answers
- Consistency of performance

**Score thresholds:**
- **80** = Proficient (good stopping point for new topics)
- **90** = Excellent (enters "Challenge Zone" — harder questions, smaller gains, bigger penalties)
- **100** = Complete mastery

The Challenge Zone (90-100) deliberately makes the final push difficult: correct answers gain 1-2 points, incorrect answers lose 3-8 points. This prevents false mastery.

### Real-Time Diagnostic

- Pinpoints grade-level proficiency in **6 maths strands** and **4 English strands**
- Diagnostic level (e.g. 400 = ready for Year 4, 450 = halfway through Year 4)
- Continuously updates as child practises — always current
- Generates **personalised action plans** with specific skill recommendations
- **Strand Analysis** groups students at similar levels for targeted teaching
- Full diagnostic takes ~45 minutes per subject; updates in as little as 10 minutes/week thereafter

### Visualisations

- **Assessment Levels graph** — shows proficiency vs grade-level expectations
- **SmartScore growth charts** — visual progression over time
- **Strand proficiency overview** — multi-strand breakdown
- **Diagnostic Levels report** — at-a-glance above/on/below grade level
- **Growth report** — starting level vs current level with overall growth visualised
- **Trouble Spots clustering** — visual grouping of problem areas

### Exam Readiness Indicator

**Not exam-specific**, but the Diagnostic Level system provides a clear "where are they relative to where they should be" indicator. The grade-level proficiency measurement effectively serves as a readiness gauge.

### Unique Features

- **Challenge Zone mechanic** — prevents false mastery, rewards genuine understanding
- **Asymmetric scoring** — harder to gain points than lose them near mastery, combating guessing
- **Continuous diagnostic** — never stale, always reflects current knowledge
- **Strand-level granularity** — not just "maths" but geometry, algebra, fractions etc.
- **Every question logged** — complete audit trail for parent review
- **Trouble Spots report** — specifically identifies what needs reteaching, not just "low scores"

### Why IXL Matters for Our Design

IXL's SmartScore is the most psychologically sophisticated scoring system in this space. It solves two problems most platforms ignore:
1. **False mastery** — getting 80% right doesn't mean you know it (could be guessing)
2. **Motivation near the top** — making the last 10% genuinely challenging prevents coasting

## 7. Tassomai (Bonus Find)

**URL:** tassomai.com | **Originally GCSE-focused, expanding to 11+**

Tassomai appeared in searches as a platform with notably good visual progress features.

### Metrics Shown

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Daily Goals completion** | How many daily goals completed per subject |
| **Understanding Grid** | Accuracy and coverage per topic |
| **Usage stats** | Time spent, questions answered |
| **Strengths and weaknesses** | Per-topic identification |

### Visualisations

- **The Tree** — an innovative visual metaphor where a tree grows as the child learns. Each subject has its own tree. Knowledge gaps are visible as sparse branches. Parents can literally "watch their child's knowledge grow."
- **Understanding Grid** — colour-coded matrix where:
  - **Dot size** = how much content the child has seen
  - **Dot colour** = accuracy (blue = highest, then green, orange, red = lowest)
- **Colour-coded columns** — green and blue = on track, orange/red = needs attention
- **Parent Dashboard** — four different views of child's progress

### Unique Features

- **The Tree metaphor** — most distinctive progress visualisation of any platform. Makes abstract progress tangible and engaging for children
- **Colour-coded accuracy grid** — two dimensions (coverage + accuracy) in a single visual
- **Daily Goal tracking** — simple metric that drives regular engagement
- **Weekly email reports** — links directly to Parent Dashboard

## 9. Parent Forum Sentiment

### What Parents Say Works (Mumsnet, Trustpilot, elevenplusexams.co.uk)

1. **"At a glance" overview** — parents want to quickly see if their child is on track without digging through screens
2. **Topic-level specificity** — knowing "maths is 72%" is useless; knowing "fractions are weak, decimals are strong" is actionable
3. **Comparison to peers** — Atom's target school comparison is consistently praised as the most valuable single feature
4. **SAS/standardised scoring** — parents want a number they can compare to school entry thresholds
5. **Time tracking** — knowing how long a child spent (and on what) helps manage the study schedule
6. **Weak area recommendations** — "what should we work on next?" is the question parents most want answered

### What Parents Say Is Missing or Broken

1. **Confusing scoring changes** — Atom changed their scoring system and parents lost trust (mastery score vs performance score confusion)
2. **No clear exam readiness signal** — many platforms show lots of data but don't answer "will my child pass?"
3. **SAS accuracy concerns** — parents report Atom SAS doesn't always predict real exam results accurately
4. **Historical data access** — hard to see long-term trends on some platforms
5. **Too much data, not enough insight** — "I can see 50 metrics but I don't know what to DO"
6. **Child motivation vs data** — some parents want to hide scores from anxious children (Atom's password protection addresses this)
7. **Speed/timing data** — parents want to know if their child is fast enough, not just accurate enough
8. **Written practice gap** — online platforms can't track paper-based practice (Atom's photo upload partially addresses this)

## 11. What Parents Say Works vs What's Missing

### The 5 Things That Matter Most to Parents

1. **"Is my child going to pass?" signal** — A single, clear readiness indicator (Atom's SAS is closest)
2. **"What should we work on next?" recommendation** — Actionable next steps, not just data
3. **"How do we compare?" benchmarking** — Against peers, against school thresholds
4. **"Are they getting faster?" speed tracking** — Accuracy alone isn't enough for timed exams
5. **"Are they actually trying?" effort tracking** — Time spent, consistency, daily engagement

### The Biggest Gap Across All Platforms

No single platform combines all five well. Atom dominates on readiness and benchmarking but lacks effort visualisation and speed tracking. IXL has the best scoring algorithm but isn't 11+ specific. Century Tech has the best effort-vs-achievement insight but no exam readiness indicator. Explore Learning has the best predictive forecasting but limited parent self-service analytics.

## 12. Design Implications for Our App

Based on this research, here are the features that would make our progress tracker genuinely best-in-class:

### Must-Have (Table Stakes)

- **Topic-level performance breakdown** — accuracy per topic, with drill-down to subtopics
- **Progress over time** — visual chart showing improvement week-by-week
- **Strengths and weaknesses** — clearly labelled, with colour coding (green/amber/red)
- **"What to work on next" recommendation** — auto-generated based on weakest areas

### Differentiators (Ahead of Atom)

- **Exam Readiness Score** — a single composite number combining accuracy, speed, coverage, and consistency. Unlike Atom's SAS (only available per mock test), this should update continuously
- **Speed tracking** — average time per question by topic, compared to GL Assessment time limits. Flag "accurate but too slow" separately from "fast but inaccurate"
- **Consistency indicator** — track whether scores are stable or volatile (a child scoring 60%, 90%, 50%, 85% is less ready than one scoring 70%, 72%, 74%, 75%)
- **Coverage map** — visual "heat map" showing which topics/question types have been practised vs untouched. Parents need to know "my child hasn't tried any algebra questions" as much as "my child got algebra questions wrong"
- **Effort dashboard** — sessions per week, time spent, streak tracking. Separate from performance so parents can see "doing the work but struggling" vs "not doing the work"

### Innovative (Nobody Does This Well)

- **The Tree (inspired by Tassomai)** — a visual metaphor that makes progress tangible for children. Could be a tree, a building, a map — something that visually "grows"
- **Parent vs Child views** — Atom's password protection is the right instinct but wrong execution. Better: a child-facing view that emphasises growth, effort, and achievements (medals, streaks) and a parent-facing view with the hard numbers (percentages, readiness scores, benchmarks)
- **Time-to-ready forecast** — Explore Learning's Compass 2.0 predicts "how long until mastery." We could predict "at current pace, estimated readiness by [exam date]"
- **Interventions quadrant (inspired by Century Tech)** — plot accuracy vs effort to identify four quadrants: (1) High effort + high accuracy = champion, (2) Low effort + high accuracy = coasting, (3) High effort + low accuracy = needs support, (4) Low effort + low accuracy = needs engagement
- **Smart distractor analysis** — track WHICH wrong answers a child picks, not just that they got it wrong. If they consistently pick the "common misconception" distractor, that's a different intervention from randomly guessing
