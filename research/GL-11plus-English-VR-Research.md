# GL Assessment 11+ Research: English & Verbal Reasoning

## Dorset Consortium Specifics (Bournemouth/Parkstone Grammar)

### Exam Structure
| Paper | Duration | Questions | Format |
|-------|----------|-----------|--------|
| Maths | 50 mins | ~50 | Multiple choice |
| English | 45 mins | ~49 | Multiple choice |
| Verbal Reasoning | 50 mins | ~80 | Multiple choice |

- **Total test time:** 2 hours 25 minutes, same day
- **Answer method:** Separate OMR answer sheet (fill bubbles)
- **No NVR paper** in Dorset
- **No creative writing** — entirely multiple choice
- All 5 options per question (A-E)

### Scoring
- Raw marks converted to **age-standardised scores (SAS)**, max 141 per paper
- Three papers equally weighted, max total ~423
- Younger children (summer born) get proportionally more credit
- **No fixed pass mark** — qualifying standard set each year based on cohort

## English Paper

### Structure (GL standard format)
| Section | Questions | Format |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Comprehension | ~13-18 | Questions on a ~500-word passage |
| Spelling | ~4-8 | Error-spotting in segmented sentences |
| Punctuation | ~4-8 | Error-spotting in segmented sentences |
| Grammar/Cloze | ~4-8 | Sentence completion (gap-fill) |

**Key insight:** Technical English (spelling + punctuation + grammar) = ~50% of marks. Comprehension = ~50%.

### Comprehension
**Passage types:** Classic fiction (Conan Doyle, Hardy, Nesbit, Carroll, Burnett), contemporary fiction, non-fiction, poetry. Reading level above chronological age.

**Question types:**
1. **Retrieval** (~40-50%) — "According to the passage, what did...?"
2. **Inference** (~20-30%) — "What does this suggest about...?"
3. **Vocabulary in context** (~15-20%) — "Which word is closest in meaning to X as used in line Y?"
4. **Literary devices** (~5-10%) — "What technique is used in the phrase...?"
5. **Word class identification** (~5-10%) — "What type of word is X?"
6. **Author's purpose/tone** — "Why does the author use...?"
7. **Summary** — "Which best summarises this passage?"

**Distractor design:** 5 options, closely related, red herrings, partially correct answers. Often 2 plausible answers — must pick the BEST one.

### Spelling (Error-Spotting Format)
Sentence divided into 4 sections (A-D). Identify which section has a spelling error. Option E = no mistake.

**Topics:** Homophones, common misspellings, double consonants, silent letters, -ible/-able, -tion/-sion, ie/ei, prefixes, suffixes, statutory Year 5/6 word list (100 words).

### Punctuation (Same Error-Spotting Format)
**Topics:** Apostrophes (possession + contraction), commas (lists, fronted adverbials, clauses), speech marks, colons, semicolons, capitals, question/exclamation marks, brackets, dashes, hyphens.

### Grammar/Cloze (Gap-Fill Format)
Sentence with a gap, 5 options to fill it.

**Topics:** Subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, correct verb forms, prepositions, pronoun agreement, homophones in context, comparative/superlative, connectives, active/passive voice, modal verbs, formal/informal register.

## English Topics for App Implementation

### 1. Reading Comprehension
- Retrieval / fact-finding
- Inference and deduction
- Vocabulary in context (synonyms)
- Literary device identification (simile, metaphor, personification, alliteration, onomatopoeia, hyperbole)
- Word class identification (noun, verb, adjective, adverb, pronoun, preposition, conjunction, determiner)
- Author's purpose and tone
- Summarising main ideas

### 2. Spelling
- Homophones (their/there/they're, where/wear/were, etc.)
- KS2 statutory word list (100 words)
- Prefixes (dis-, un-, re-, mis-, over-, de-, pre-, anti-)
- Suffixes (-tion/-sion/-cian, -ous/-ious, -ible/-able, -ful/-less, -ment/-ness, -ly)
- Silent letters (knife, write, gnaw, psalm, receipt, subtle, doubt)
- Double consonants (accommodate, recommend, necessary, occasion, embarrass)
- Common misspellings (separate, definitely, environment, government, rhythm)
- Word endings (-ence/-ance, -ent/-ant, -er/-or/-ar)
- Plurals (regular, irregular, -y, -f/-fe, -o)

### 3. Punctuation
- Full stops and capital letters
- Commas (lists, clauses, fronted adverbials, clarity)
- Apostrophes (contraction and possession)
- Speech marks / inverted commas
- Question marks and exclamation marks
- Colons and semicolons
- Brackets, dashes, and hyphens

### 4. Grammar
- Word classes (all 8)
- Verb tenses (past, present, future; simple, progressive, perfect)
- Subject-verb agreement
- Active and passive voice
- Modal verbs (might, should, will, must, could, would)
- Relative clauses (who, which, where, when, whose, that)
- Formal vs informal register
- Subjunctive forms
- Fronted adverbials
- Expanded noun phrases
- Cohesion and linking devices

### 5. Vocabulary
- Synonyms, antonyms, words in context
- Word roots (Latin/Greek)
- Prefixes changing meaning, suffixes changing word class

## Verbal Reasoning Paper

### Format
- 80 questions in 50 minutes (~37 seconds per question)
- Questions **grouped by type** (not mixed)
- Each type has instructions + worked example before questions begin
- Typically 3-5 questions per type section
- 21 core types; not all appear in every paper

### All 21 Question Types

#### CODE & PATTERN (Types 1-3)
| # | Name | Example | Difficulty | Vocab Demand |
|---|------|---------|------------|--------------|
| 1 | Crack the Letter Code | LUCK=JSAI, what does EMMB mean? | Medium-Hard | None |
| 2 | Complete the Letter Series | AZ, BY, CX, DW, EV, ? | Medium-Hard | None |
| 3 | Complete the Letter Sentence | QQ is to NK as HH is to ? | Medium | None |

#### WORD MANIPULATION (Types 4-8)
| # | Name | Example | Difficulty | Vocab Demand |
|---|------|---------|------------|--------------|

| 6 | Move a Letter | beacon + vent → bacon + event | Medium-Hard | High |
| 7 | Find Missing Three-Letter Word | GA___TED → HER (GATHERED) | Medium | High |

### Essential Skills
- **EJOTY mnemonic:** E=5, J=10, O=15, T=20, Y=25 (alphabet positions)
- Alphabet forwards AND backwards fluently
- Alphabet as continuous loop (Z wraps to A)
- Mirror pairs: A-Z, B-Y, C-X, D-W, E-V, F-U, G-T, H-S, I-R, J-Q, K-P, L-O, M-N
- Mental arithmetic (no BIDMAS in letter sums — work left to right)
