## Overview

Nessy is a UK-founded structured literacy programme designed primarily for children with dyslexia or reading and spelling difficulties, aged 5–12. Its flagship product, Nessy Reading and Spelling, uses a multisensory approach (Orton-Gillingham methodology) to teach phonics, decoding, and spelling through games, animated characters, and decodable books. Companion products include Hairy Reading (a structured phonics programme with decodable books) and Number Sense by Chris Woodin (a programme for dyscalculia and maths learning difficulties). Nessy occupies a specialist niche — it is not a general-purpose primary edtech app but a targeted intervention tool — which justifies its premium pricing relative to the market. It is used in homes and schools across the UK, US, and Australia.

## Free vs paid

- A **free trial** is referenced on the Nessy UK site, though duration is not confirmed on the pages accessible at verification date.
- The homepage for each product includes a free trial entry point, suggesting trial access is available before purchase.
- There is no permanent free tier; all learning content is paywalled.
- The entry price of ~£10.50/month (monthly, single child) or £76.50/year (annual, single child) positions Nessy as a mid-to-premium product — noticeably more expensive than general edtech but less expensive than specialist dyslexia tutoring.

## Family / sibling pricing

Nessy uses a volume-discount per-child model rather than a flat family bundle. The per-child price falls steeply with each additional child:

- 1st child: £76.50/year
- 2nd child: £51.00/year (33% discount vs first child)
- 3rd child: £38.50/year (50% discount vs first child)
- 4th+ children: £32.00/year each (58% discount vs first child)

For a family with two children who both have identified literacy difficulties, the second-child discount is meaningful. However, the model implicitly assumes that most Nessy families have only one child with a learning need — a reasonable assumption given Nessy's specialist positioning.

## Schools licensing (if applicable)

Nessy operates a separate school and specialist teacher licensing programme. School pricing is not published on the consumer site; schools and SENDCos contact Nessy directly. School licences typically cover a defined number of pupils and may be accessed through local authority procurement frameworks. The school product includes teacher progress reports, class management tools, and access to Nessy's professional development resources. Nessy is widely recommended by SENCOs and educational psychologists, which drives school adoption.

## Unusual mechanics

- **Specialist positioning:** Nessy is the only product in this comparison set with an explicit dyslexia/SEND focus. This positioning justifies a higher per-child price and insulates it from direct competition on price with general-purpose apps.
- **Volume discount per child:** The steep discount curve (33%–58% off for additional children) rewards large families while capturing maximum value from single-child households.
- **Orton-Gillingham methodology:** The underlying teaching method is evidence-based and named, which is a credibility and trust signal for parents who have researched intervention programmes.
- **Chris Woodin Number Sense product:** The co-branded maths product adds a second revenue line and expands the product's scope without diluting the specialist brand.
- **Professional recommendation pathway:** Many Nessy subscribers are referred by educational psychologists, SENCOs, or paediatricians — a very different acquisition channel from general consumer marketing, which means the product faces little price resistance at the point of purchase.
- **No mentioned gifting or lifetime plans** in publicly available information.

## Applicability to UK 11+ app pricing

Nessy's per-child volume discount model is the most interesting structural option for a 11+ app with a multi-child use case (e.g. siblings preparing for the same exam 2 years apart). It preserves premium per-child value whilst offering a meaningful loyalty incentive for returning families. At £76.50/year for one child, Nessy also demonstrates that UK parents preparing children for high-stakes educational outcomes will pay a specialist premium — relevant context for 11+ pricing, where parental anxiety about outcomes is similarly elevated.
